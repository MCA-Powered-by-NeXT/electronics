Week 1 Detailed Pacing Model

Week One may feel slower than later weeks because students are learning how to work, not just what to know. Protect that time. Strong safety, vocabulary, simulation, and documentation habits make every later lab more efficient.

Recommended Sequence

WeekCore FocusSuggested TimeNotes
1Safety, technician role, electricity basics, and Tinkercad simulation10-15 hrsDo not rush safety culture. Use inspection practice and simulation before physical circuits.
2Voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's Law, LED circuit10-15 hrsKeep math simple and use physical examples.
3DMM and continuity10-15 hrsRequire repeated meter setup checks.
4Components, schematics, series/parallel10-15 hrsUse component trays and schematic cards.
5Workmanship and troubleshooting10-15 hrsUse controlled low-voltage fault boards only.
6Final project and certificate checkoff10-15 hrsReserve time for retries and documentation cleanup.

60-Hour Version

Prioritize safety, Tinkercad circuit reasoning, DMM setup, continuity, simple circuits, schematics, troubleshooting, and final project completion. Treat transistor, logic, soldering, and capacitor theory as short demonstrations only if time allows.

75-Hour Version

Add more guided practice for simulation comparisons, resistor color codes, schematic tracing, series/parallel comparisons, repair ticket writing, and fault-board rotations. Include more student presentations during Week 6.

90-Hour Version

Include deeper enrichment such as diodes, capacitors, transistor switching, logic gates, simulation variations, and workmanship inspection while maintaining the low-voltage safety boundary.

Make-Up and Retry Guidance

  • Safety-critical checkoffs must be retried after coaching if unsafe behavior occurs.
  • Allow quiz corrections as review, but keep practical pass/retry standards clear.
  • Students who miss a lab should complete a supervised make-up station, not only a written substitute.
  • Students who miss the Tinkercad lesson should complete a guided simulation or instructor-observed demonstration before Week Two circuits.
  • Document all practical retries so completion decisions are transparent.
