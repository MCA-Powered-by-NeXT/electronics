Recommended Sequence

WeekCore FocusSuggested TimeNotes
1Safety, technician role, electricity basics10-15 hrsDo not rush safety culture. Use inspection practice before circuits.
2Voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's Law, LED circuit10-15 hrsKeep math simple and use physical examples.
3DMM and continuity10-15 hrsRequire repeated meter setup checks.
4Components, schematics, series/parallel10-15 hrsUse component trays and schematic cards.
5Workmanship and troubleshooting10-15 hrsUse controlled low-voltage fault boards only.
6Final project and certificate checkoff10-15 hrsReserve time for retries and documentation cleanup.

60-Hour Version

Keep transistors, logic, soldering, and capacitor theory as short enrichment demonstrations. Prioritize safety, DMM setup, continuity, simple circuits, schematics, troubleshooting, and final project completion.

75-Hour Version

Add more guided practice for resistor color codes, schematic tracing, series/parallel comparisons, repair ticket writing, and fault-board rotations.

90-Hour Version

Include deeper enrichment from legacy Days 4-8, such as diodes, capacitors, transistor switching, logic gates, and solder joint inspection, while maintaining the low-voltage safety boundary.

Make-Up and Retry Guidance

  • Safety-critical checkoffs must be retried after coaching if unsafe behavior occurs.
  • Allow quiz corrections as review, but keep practical pass/retry standards clear.
  • Students who miss a lab should complete a supervised make-up station, not just a written substitute.
