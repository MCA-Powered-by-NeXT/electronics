Daily Quiz Answer Keys

Instructor-facing suggested answer themes for the student 10-question daily lesson quizzes.

How To Use These Keys

These are suggested answer themes, not the only acceptable wording. Award credit for accurate student explanations using the vocabulary and safety habits taught in the lesson. Student-facing quiz pages do not include answers.

Week 1 Answer Themes

Day 1

  1. Pause, inspect, control hazards, then work.
  2. Clear bench, dry area, good tools/leads, assigned source.
  3. Personal protective equipment; protects eyes/body during lab.
  4. Unclear power state means stop and ask.
  5. Damaged lead, exposed conductor, clutter, liquid, wrong source.
  6. Careful work follows procedure; guessing skips evidence.
  7. Documentation records safety decisions and observations.
  8. Disconnect or control source and ask if unsure.
  9. Reduces accidental contact, confusion, and lost parts.
  10. Accept any safe habit taught in Day 1.

Day 2

  1. Computer model of a circuit.
  2. Practice safely and predict behavior before physical build.
  3. Battery/source, resistor, LED, switch, wires.
  4. Runs the virtual circuit.
  5. State expected result before testing.
  6. Path opens; output should stop.
  7. Limits current through LED.
  8. Record symptom and likely path/polarity/value issue.
  9. Builds safe sequence: inspect, predict, test, explain.
  10. Source to switch/resistor/LED and back to return.

Day 3

  1. Source supplies, path carries, load uses, return completes.
  2. Unbroken intended route from source through load back to source.
  3. Break or gap in intended path.
  4. Unwanted route that bypasses intended load/control.
  5. No complete route means no intended flow.
  6. Material that resists flow.
  7. Material that allows flow.
  8. Opens or closes the path.
  9. Completes the loop back to source.
  10. Must name all four parts accurately.

Day 4

  1. Find hazards before work begins.
  2. PPE, tools, source, wires, bench, parts.
  3. Damaged tools can create unsafe or unreliable work.
  4. Stop, report, replace or correct with approval.
  5. Prevents powering an unsafe or miswired circuit.
  6. Safety glasses or instructor-required PPE.
  7. Liquids increase hazard and damage risk.
  8. Stop and ask instructor.
  9. What was checked, result, correction, signoff.
  10. Any appropriate bench/tool/source correction.

Day 5

  1. Work only inside safety boundary and stop when unsure.
  2. Lets students practice and observe before physical build.
  3. Source, path, load, return.
  4. Open path has a break; complete path is continuous.
  5. Prevents accidental operation and mistakes.
  6. Measurements, observations, faults, corrections, verification.
  7. Accept hazard plus safe response.
  8. Use only assigned training materials and sources.
  9. Safety, circuit loop, documentation, or simulation.
  10. Student-specific question.

Week 2 Answer Themes

Day 1

  1. Electrical push or potential.
  2. Flow through a complete path.
  3. Opposition to current.
  4. Volts.
  5. Ohms.
  6. Flow requires a loop.
  7. DC stays one direction/polarity; AC changes direction.
  8. Assigned battery or approved DC trainer.
  9. Type, voltage, polarity, approval/status.
  10. Must use the four-part model correctly.

Day 2

  1. Limits current or sets resistance.
  2. Protects LED by limiting current.
  3. First digit.
  4. Second digit.
  5. Scale factor.
  6. Allowed variation from marked value.
  7. Units make notes clear.
  8. Wrong value can cause failure or confusion.
  9. Use tolerance band/spacing and good light.
  10. Color bands, value, tolerance, where used.

Day 3

  1. V = I x R.
  2. Voltage.
  3. Current.
  4. Resistance.
  5. Units identify the quantity.
  6. Reduces current to a safe level for LED.
  7. LED can fail or circuit can behave incorrectly.
  8. Physical wiring still must be checked.
  9. Identify knowns and what is being asked.
  10. More resistance generally means less current for same voltage.

Day 4

  1. Source, polarity, wiring, resistor, LED, bench.
  2. LED works in one intended direction.
  3. In series with the LED path.
  4. DC voltage.
  5. Prevents accidental operation and wiring mistakes.
  6. Value, unit, polarity/note.
  7. Open path, reversed LED, missing resistor, weak source, wrong row.
  8. Disconnect source, inspect, trace path.
  9. Observe correct operation and record evidence.
  10. Accept complete note with source/path/load/result.

Day 5

  1. V volts, A amps, ohms resistance, W watts.
  2. Beginner safety and predictable classroom practice.
  3. LED/current path.
  4. How many zeros or scale factor.
  5. Instructor checks student setup/work.
  6. Allowed variation.
  7. Find errors before operation.
  8. Reversed LED, open path, missing resistor, wrong row.
  9. Units, safe setup, source knowledge.
  10. Student-specific reflection.

Week 3 Answer Themes

Day 1

  1. Display, dial, jacks, leads/probes.
  2. COM.
  3. Wrong jack creates wrong/unsafe setup.
  4. Meter must match measurement type.
  5. Task, source state, leads, setting, probes.
  6. Special setup and instructor approval needed.
  7. Keeping probe tips steady and away from unintended contact.
  8. Units give meaning.
  9. Stop and correct/ask.
  10. Accept complete setup routine.

Day 2

  1. DC voltage.
  2. With power disconnected/part isolated.
  3. Meter supplies test signal and powered circuits mislead/damage meter.
  4. Number, unit, item, meaning.
  5. Probe polarity reversed.
  6. Predicted/marked value compared with actual reading.
  7. Stable readings are more reliable.
  8. Wrong function/jack or poor probe contact.
  9. Measuring powered component.
  10. Accept complete note with units.

Day 3

  1. Complete conductive path.
  2. Safe and accurate only when unpowered.
  3. Continuous/low resistance/beep if meter uses beep.
  4. Open/no continuity.
  5. Continuity/low resistance.
  6. Protective part that opens path in overcurrent.
  7. Fault occurred; investigate cause.
  8. Result only matters if endpoints are known.
  9. It locates where path stops.
  10. Accept endpoint/result/note.

Day 4

  1. Demonstrate safe DMM use.
  2. What measurement is being made.
  3. COM and voltage jack, DC voltage function.
  4. Power off, resistance function, correct jacks.
  5. Power off, continuity/ohms setting.
  6. Reading, unit, item, meaning.
  7. Stop, coach, retry.
  8. Safe process matters more than lucky answer.
  9. Student-specific.
  10. Accept safe repeated habit.

Day 5

  1. Voltage checks potential across points; continuity checks path with power off.
  2. Resistance gives value; continuity gives path status.
  3. Both must match task.
  4. Special-use and setup risk.
  5. Meter becomes part of path.
  6. DC voltage.
  7. Continuity.
  8. Reading, unit, item, meaning.
  9. Trace and test paths.
  10. Student-specific safe habit.

Week 4 Answer Themes

Day 1

  1. Resistor, LED, switch, fuse, capacitor, diode, wire.
  2. Limits current/sets resistance.
  3. Produces light.
  4. LED orientation matters.
  5. Opens/closes circuit path.
  6. Opens path during fault condition.
  7. Neat routing, seated wires, organized colors.
  8. Color helps but does not prove path.
  9. Forcing leads, ignoring polarity, messy wiring.
  10. Part name, purpose, polarity/handling note.

Day 2

  1. Map of electrical connections.
  2. Shows connections, not exact layout.
  3. Resistor label.
  4. Switch label.
  5. LED label.
  6. Ensures complete intended path.
  7. Shared connection point.
  8. They identify parts on diagram.
  9. Forgetting return or treating layout as exact picture.
  10. Correct path description.

Day 3

  1. Connect real parts to drawings.
  2. Prevents orientation mistakes.
  3. Name, purpose, polarity/notes.
  4. Symbol and reference label.
  5. Shows real connection points.
  6. Safety and accuracy.
  7. Specific, includes part/path/result.
  8. Student-specific.
  9. Shows where to test.
  10. Accept correct part-symbol note.

Day 4

  1. One path.
  2. More than one branch/path.
  3. Whole path may stop.
  4. Other branch still has path.
  5. Separate path between shared points.
  6. Builds reasoning before testing.
  7. Disconnect/control source.
  8. Find break in path.
  9. Assuming two loads always means parallel.
  10. Correct drawing or description.

Day 5

  1. Accept four correct components and purposes.
  2. Electrical connections.
  3. Physical placement differs from electrical relationship.
  4. Whether a path is complete.
  5. Safety and correct reading.
  6. One path vs multiple paths.
  7. Endpoints, result, interpretation.
  8. Helps know what to test and replace.
  9. Path tracing, continuity, symbols.
  10. Student-specific.

Day 5
