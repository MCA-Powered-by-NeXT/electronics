Instructor Only

Adjust quantities based on class size. A useful planning unit is one kit per two students plus instructor demonstration spares. Keep instructor-only fault parts and answer keys separate from student materials.

Components and Consumables

  • LEDs in several colors.
  • Resistors: 220 ohm, 330 ohm, 470 ohm, 1 k ohm, 4.7 k ohm, 10 k ohm.
  • Momentary, slide, and toggle switches.
  • Training fuses and fuse holders.
  • Jumper wires and intentionally damaged wire samples for inspection only.
  • Simple buzzers or lamps for final project options.
  • Connectors, terminals, and loose-connection samples for Week 5/6 inspection.
  • Printed lab sheets, quiz pages, checkoff forms, repair tickets, final project packets, rubric sheets, and certificate checklists.

Reset and Maintenance Notes

  • Sort damaged LEDs, opened fuses, and suspect wires into a clearly marked instructor-only bin.
  • Check batteries before labs that rely on visible LED brightness.
  • Keep daily quiz answer keys separate from student quiz pages.
  • Keep fault-board fault maps private.
  • Remove any source or tool that creates uncertainty about the low-voltage boundary.
  • Inventory final project kits before Week 6 Day 1 and after Week 6 Day 5.
