Instructor Only

Use only low-voltage DC trainer boards. Do not plant faults involving wall power, high voltage, charged capacitors, vehicle systems, or unknown equipment. The goal is to assess process: symptom, safety, evidence, correction, verification, and documentation.

Recommended Training Board

A simple fault board can include a battery holder or approved trainer source, fuse holder or training fuse, switch, current-limiting resistor, LED or buzzer load, connector/terminal point, and labeled test points.

BT1 + -> F1 -> SW1 -> R1 -> LED1 -> BT1 -
Test points: TP1 source, TP2 after fuse, TP3 after switch, TP4 load return

Week 5 Checkoff Evidence

  • Student states observed symptom before naming a cause.
  • Student identifies expected operation and source-control-load-return path.
  • Student controls power before touching wiring or using continuity/resistance.
  • Student collects one useful piece of evidence before correction.
  • Student changes one thing at a time.
  • Student verifies operation after correction.
  • Student repair ticket includes symptom, safety, evidence, correction, and verification.

Reset Checklist

  • [ ] Power disconnected.
  • [ ] Fault selected and recorded privately.
  • [ ] Board inspected for accidental unsafe shorts.
  • [ ] Expected symptom verified before student use.
  • [ ] Expected measurement/continuity evidence verified.
  • [ ] Student repair ticket ready.
  • [ ] Fault reset after each group.
