Instructor Only

Use this page to prepare benches, tools, parts, and safety controls before students arrive. All labs must remain within the low-voltage DC training boundary.

Week 1 Lab 1 Setup: Safety Inspection and PPE

Prepare at least one staged workstation per team or rotate teams through a shared station. All staged hazards must be safe props. Do not create a real energized hazard.

Station ItemHow to Stage SafelyWhat Students Should Notice
Safety glassesPlace nearby but not worn by mannequin/photo card/student volunteerPPE must be selected before work.
Disconnected battery holderLeave battery removed or lead disconnectedPower state must be checked before work.
Damaged sample wireUse a clearly disconnected scrap wire with nicked insulationDamaged insulation should be removed from service.
Cluttered jumper leadsScatter extra leads around the breadboardClutter hides circuit paths and can create accidental contact.
Cup propUse an empty cup or photo card, never liquid near electronicsLiquids do not belong at the bench.
DMM with lead placement issueUse a powered-off meter as a visual prop onlyLead jacks must match the measurement task.
Unlabeled parts trayMix a small number of safe parts or labelsUnclear parts and labels slow down safe work.

General Safety Setup

  • Use only low-voltage battery-powered DC training circuits.
  • Remove access to wall outlets as measurement points.
  • Inspect DMM leads for damaged insulation.
  • Use current-limiting resistors for all LED circuits.
  • Keep spare batteries and components separated from damaged or failed parts.
  • Label instructor-only failed parts so students do not accidentally use them in normal builds.
