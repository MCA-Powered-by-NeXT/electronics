Instructor Only

These are answer-key notes, not full scripts. Accept equivalent wording when the safety concept and technical idea are correct.

Week 1 Key Notes

  • Power should be disconnected before rewiring.
  • PPE includes safety glasses for this course.
  • Unknown circuits are treated as potentially energized.
  • Source provides energy, path carries current, load uses energy, return completes the circuit.

Week 2 Key Notes

  • Voltage is electrical push; current is flow; resistance limits flow.
  • Ohm's Law: I = V/R. For 9 V and 1000 ohm, current is 0.009 A or 9 mA.
  • Yellow-violet-red-gold is 47 x 100 = 4700 ohm or 4.7 k ohm, plus or minus 5%.
  • Student labs are low-voltage DC only.

Week 3 Key Notes

  • Resistance and continuity require power off.
  • Black lead normally goes in common.
  • No beep through a fuse usually indicates open fuse or poor probe contact.
  • Negative DC voltage often means the probes are reversed.

Week 4 Key Notes

  • Fuses provide protection and should not be bypassed.
  • Schematics show electrical function, not exact physical placement.
  • Series circuits have one path; parallel circuits have multiple branches.
  • A filled junction dot usually means wires connect.

Week 5 Key Notes

  • Troubleshooting starts by stating the symptom.
  • Make one correction at a time to preserve the evidence trail.
  • A fuse that opens again requires root-cause investigation.
  • Repair tickets should include symptom, tests, expected and actual results, correction, and final verification.

Week 6 Key Notes

  • Strong final evidence includes safe inspection, measurements, continuity checks, function demonstration, and documentation.
  • For 9 V and 470 ohm, current is about 0.019 A or 19 mA.
  • Certificate completion requires safety, DMM, continuity, circuit building, troubleshooting, documentation, and final project demonstration.
