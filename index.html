Course Overview

This site is now organized as a larger Basic Electronics Technician course. The sequence builds from safety and electrical foundations to DMM use, continuity testing, schematics, circuit building, troubleshooting, documentation, and final project demonstration.

Safety boundary: Student labs use low-voltage battery-powered DC training circuits only. Students must not work on wall outlets, mains-powered devices, open power supplies, vehicle electrical systems, or unknown energized equipment.

Weeks

Week 1

Electrical safety and technician fundamentals.

Week 2

Voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's Law, batteries, and LED circuits.

Week 3

DMM safety, measurement skills, continuity, switches, and fuses.

Week 4

Components, wiring, schematic reading, series and parallel circuits.

Week 5

Applied control, workmanship, and systematic troubleshooting.

Week 6

Final project, documentation, career pathways, and certificate checkoff.
