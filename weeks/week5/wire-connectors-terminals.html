Week 5 Support: Wire Identification, Connectors, and Terminals

Supplemental classroom content for wire identification, connectors, and terminals.

Week 5Day 1Day 2Day 3

Purpose

This page adds focused coverage for wire identification, connectors, and terminals. These topics support troubleshooting because many faults are caused by loose, damaged, mislabeled, or incorrectly terminated wiring.

Learning Targets

  • Identify common wire characteristics: color, size, insulation condition, solid or stranded type, and label.
  • Identify common connector and terminal features.
  • Inspect wire and terminal condition before troubleshooting.
  • Explain how poor termination can create intermittent faults.
  • Document a wiring or connector issue clearly.

Wire Identification

FeatureWhat To ObserveWhy It Matters
ColorRed, black, or other insulation colors.Can help organize source and return, but does not prove connection.
SizeRelative conductor thickness or printed size if available.Wire must fit the connector and training circuit.
Solid or strandedSingle conductor or many fine strands.Affects breadboard use, flexibility, and termination.
Insulation conditionCracks, cuts, or exposed conductor.Damage can cause unreliable operation.
LabelingTags, numbers, or documented circuit locations.Helps tracing and troubleshooting.

Connectors and Terminals

ItemBeginner MeaningInspection Questions
TerminalA point where a wire or component connects.Is the conductor secure? Is extra metal showing?
ConnectorA removable or fixed part that joins conductors.Is it seated? Is it cracked, loose, or mismatched?
Screw terminalA terminal tightened by a screw.Is the conductor held correctly?
Crimp terminalA terminal mechanically attached to a wire.Is the crimp secure? Are strands loose or broken?
Breadboard connectionA temporary spring contact inside a breadboard row.Is the wire fully seated in the correct row?

Inspection Lab Add-On

  1. Inspect three wire samples and record color, condition, and type.
  2. Inspect two connectors or terminals and record whether they are secure.
  3. Use continuity testing only with power off to verify a wire or connector path.
  4. Write one technician note describing a wiring issue and safe corrective action.

Connector Fault Examples

SymptomPossible Wiring / Connector CauseSafe First Check

Circuit works sometimes.Intermittent connection or damaged wire.Inspect wire and connector condition.
Output is inconsistent.Connector not fully seated.Disconnect source and inspect connector area.
