Student Reading: Learn This First

The checkoff grades process. The goal is not to guess quickly. The goal is to troubleshoot safely and explain the evidence.

Speak the process aloud. First state the symptom. Then state expected operation. Then control power and inspect.

Choose one test or inspection target. The test should match the question. Continuity is power-off. Voltage is only on assigned low-voltage points. Visual inspection is often first.

After collecting evidence, make one correction. One correction at a time protects the evidence trail.

Then verify. Verification means the circuit now performs the expected operation, not just that you changed something.

End with the checkoff note. It should include symptom, expected operation, evidence, correction, and verification.

Lesson Work

  1. Complete a troubleshooting scenario under observation.
  2. State symptom and expected operation.
  3. Collect one useful piece of evidence safely.
  4. Correct one issue and verify operation.
  5. Submit a repair ticket.
