Week 5 Day 4: Troubleshooting Practical Checkoff

2-3 hour class block for demonstrating troubleshooting process and documentation.

Week 5Previous DayNext Day

Day 4 Purpose

Students demonstrate that they can troubleshoot safely and systematically, not by guessing. The instructor evaluates process, safety, evidence, correction, verification, and documentation.

Learning Targets

  • State the symptom clearly.
  • Make the circuit safe before changes.
  • Use source-path-load-return reasoning.
  • Use measurement or continuity evidence appropriately.
  • Correct one issue at a time.
  • Verify and document the result.

Checkoff Scenario Script

  1. Student states the observed symptom.
  2. Student identifies the power state and makes the circuit safe.
  3. Student visually inspects workmanship and path.
  4. Student chooses one test or inspection step.
  5. Student records evidence.
  6. Student corrects one approved fault.
  7. Student verifies operation.
  8. Student completes a repair note.

Retry and Coaching Notes

If the student misses a safety step, stop the checkoff and reteach before retry. If the student reaches the correct answer by guessing, require a second scenario where they must state evidence before correction.

Reflection

What evidence did you use before correcting the fault?

What troubleshooting step will you use first next time?

What documentation habit improved today?
