Student Reading: Learn This First

Control means changing whether a circuit path is complete. A switch, pushbutton, connector, or jumper can control when a load receives current.

Identify the control in a simple circuit. Ask what happens when it is open and what happens when it is closed. The answer should be about the path, not just the output.

Workmanship is the physical quality of the build. A circuit can be correct in theory but unreliable if wires are loose, terminals are weak, or paths are hidden.

Inspect workmanship as evidence. A loose connector can create intermittent operation. A strained lead can fail later. A poorly routed wire can hide a wrong connection.

Color coding helps but does not prove connection. Trace endpoints and check that the wire actually connects the intended points.

Write a workmanship note: condition observed, likely electrical effect, and safe correction.

Lesson Work

  1. Identify control devices in simple circuits.
  2. Inspect wire, connector, and terminal samples.
  3. Describe possible symptoms caused by poor workmanship.
  4. Write workmanship notes with observation, effect, and correction.
