Week 5 Day 1: Applied Control and Workmanship

2-3 hour class block connecting switches, control ideas, and visible workmanship inspection.

Week 5Previous WeekNext Day

Day 1 Purpose

Students learn that many circuits are controlled by switches or control paths, and that workmanship affects reliability. Clean work is easier to inspect, test, and troubleshoot. This day prepares students to notice visible problems before reaching for a meter.

Learning Targets

  • Explain how switches control a circuit path.
  • Recognize basic control ideas such as input, output, and switch-controlled loads.
  • Identify visible workmanship concerns.
  • Explain why workmanship affects troubleshooting.
  • Write an inspection note that describes a visible issue and a likely effect.

Control Vocabulary

TermBeginner MeaningExample
InputA signal or action that tells a circuit what to do.Pressing a switch.
OutputThe visible or useful result.LED turns on.
Control pathThe part of the circuit that decides whether the load operates.Switch in series with LED.
Intermittent faultA problem that appears and disappears.Loose wire flickers LED.
