Week 5 Day 1: Applied Control and Workmanship

2-3 hour class block connecting switches, control ideas, and visible workmanship inspection.

Week 5Previous WeekNext Day

Day 1 Purpose

Students learn that many circuits are controlled by switches or control paths, and that workmanship affects reliability. Clean work is easier to inspect, test, and troubleshoot.

Learning Targets

  • Explain how switches control a circuit path.
  • Recognize basic control ideas such as pull-up or transistor-as-switch exposure.
  • Identify visible workmanship concerns.
  • Explain why workmanship affects troubleshooting.

Technician Prompt

Inspect a prepared example. Write the symptom, the visible workmanship issue, why it matters, and the safest next action.

Knowledge Check

1. How does a switch control a circuit?
2. Why does neat wiring help troubleshooting?
3. What is one visible workmanship issue?
4. Why should technicians document what they see before changing anything?
