Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how switches, pull-ups, and transistor control appear in simple circuits.
  • Identify visible workmanship issues in prepared examples.
  • Use a troubleshooting sequence to separate power, connection, component, and wiring faults.
  • Complete a simple repair ticket with measurements and evidence.

Instructor Notes

Fault boards should use low-voltage DC only and resettable faults such as missing jumpers, reversed LEDs, open switches, blown training fuses, or wrong-value resistors.
