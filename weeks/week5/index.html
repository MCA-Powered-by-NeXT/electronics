Week 5: Applied Control, Workmanship, and Systematic Troubleshooting

Students connect control ideas to practical technician work: switches, transistor-as-switch exposure, pull-ups, workmanship inspection, fault symptoms, and repair evidence.

Module Description

Students move from building known-good circuits to diagnosing unknown faults. The goal is not guessing; it is a repeatable process: observe, isolate, measure, correct, verify, and document.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how switches, pull-ups, and transistor control appear in simple circuits.
  • Identify visible workmanship issues such as loose wires, poor seating, and solder bridges in prepared examples.
  • Use a troubleshooting sequence to separate power, connection, component, and wiring faults.
  • Complete a simple repair ticket with measurements and evidence.
