Week Five Purpose

Week Five moves students from known-good builds into diagnosis. Students learn to observe symptoms, inspect wiring and connectors, isolate likely causes, measure safely, correct one thing at a time, verify the result, and document evidence.

This week deliberately uses small beginner electronics examples: switch-controlled LEDs, battery/source paths, resistors in current paths, simple branches, connectors, terminals, and physical wire condition. These match the kind of beginner component examples shown in the uploaded Forrest Mims electronics PDF, but the classroom activities stay within the course safety boundary: assigned low-voltage DC trainers only.

Safety boundary: Troubleshooting activities use low-voltage DC fault boards or instructor-approved training circuits only. Students disconnect power before rewiring, continuity testing, resistance testing, or moving components.

Week 5 Skill Progression

  1. Day 1: Students inspect control paths, switches, wires, connectors, terminals, and workmanship before using a meter.
  2. Day 2: Students learn the eight-step troubleshooting process and practice identifying symptom, evidence, cause, correction, and verification.
  3. Day 3: Students use the process on prepared low-voltage fault boards and complete technician-style repair tickets.
  4. Day 4: Students demonstrate safe troubleshooting in a practical checkoff.
  5. Day 5: Students review, take the quiz, clean up documentation, and connect troubleshooting to the final project.

Required Examples and Materials

  • Switch-controlled LED training circuit with resistor and battery or approved DC trainer.
  • Known-good and prepared-fault versions of the same simple circuit.
  • Wire samples: solid jumper, stranded lead, damaged insulation sample, long-stripped sample, labeled sample.
  • Connector/terminal samples: breadboard rows, battery clip, screw terminal or terminal strip, plug connector if available.
  • Prepared faults: missing return, reversed LED, wrong switch terminals, loose jumper, wrong resistor row, unseated connector, exposed conductor risk.
  • Student repair ticket forms and checkoff rubric.

Week Five Completion Checklist
