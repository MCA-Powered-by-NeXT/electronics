Week 5: Applied Control, Workmanship, Wiring, and Systematic Troubleshooting

Five daily class meetings, 2-3 hours each. Students learn control ideas, inspect workmanship, identify wire/connector/terminal issues, use a troubleshooting process, and complete a practical checkoff.

Week Five Purpose

Week Five moves students from known-good builds into diagnosis. Students learn to observe symptoms, inspect wiring and connectors, isolate likely causes, measure safely, correct one thing at a time, verify the result, and document evidence.

Safety boundary: Troubleshooting activities use low-voltage DC fault boards or instructor-approved training circuits only.
