Week 5 Day 3: Fault Board Troubleshooting Lab

2-3 hour class block for low-voltage fault-board diagnosis and repair ticket writing.

Week 5Previous DayNext Day

Day 3 Purpose

Students apply troubleshooting steps to controlled low-voltage faults. The emphasis is safe process, evidence, and clear documentation. This lab should feel like a technician work order: observe, test, correct, verify, document.

Lab safety: Use only instructor-approved fault boards. Disconnect power before moving wires or components.

Learning Targets

  • Observe and describe a fault symptom.
  • Use safe checks to isolate a likely cause.
  • Correct one fault under instructor rules.
  • Complete a repair ticket that includes evidence and verification.
  • Explain the difference between the original symptom and the confirmed cause.

Lab Safety Checklist

  • [ ] Work only on assigned fault board.
  • [ ] Inspect before touching.
  • [ ] Identify the source and power state.
  • [ ] Disconnect power before moving wires.
  • [ ] Use DMM setup checklist before measuring.
  • [ ] Change one thing at a time.
  • [ ] Verify and document final result.

Common Faults

  • Missing jumper
  • Open switch
  • Reversed LED
  • Weak or disconnected source
  • Wrong resistor value
  • Loose return path
  • Broken or misplaced breadboard connection

Instructor Coaching Prompts

  • What is the symptom, not the guessed cause?
  • Which section of the circuit are you testing?
  • What evidence did that test give you?
  • What changed after your correction?
  • How would another technician know what you did?

Knowledge Check and Exit Ticket

1. What symptom did you observe first?
2. What safety step came before changing the circuit?
3. What evidence supported your diagnosis?
4. How did you verify the correction?
Exit ticket: Write one complete repair-ticket sentence.
