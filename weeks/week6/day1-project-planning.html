Week 6 Day 1: Final Project Planning

2-3 hour class block for selecting or receiving an approved final project and planning safe work.

Week 6Previous WeekNext Day

Day 1 Purpose

Students prepare for the final project by choosing an approved low-voltage project, identifying required parts, planning the circuit path, and writing a safety and test plan. The planning day should prevent random building and make the final project feel like technician work.

Safety boundary: Final projects must be approved low-voltage DC projects. Students do not change project scope without instructor approval.

Learning Targets

  • Select or receive an approved low-voltage final project.
  • Create a parts list and simple circuit plan.
  • Identify source, path, load, return, and control point.
  • Write safety checks before building.
  • Plan how the final project will be tested, verified, and documented.

Project Options

  • LED lighting circuit with switch and current-limiting resistor.
  • Continuity tester board.
  • Basic alarm circuit using low-voltage buzzer and switch.
  • Approved training board repair or fault demonstration.
  • Instructor-approved variation using the same low-voltage safety rules.

Planning Questions

  • What should the project do when it works correctly?
  • What source will power it?
  • What component or section controls the output?
  • What measurement or continuity check could verify the build?
  • What is the most likely mistake someone might make while building it?
