Week Six Purpose

Week Six is performance-based. Students demonstrate safe work habits, circuit understanding, DMM use, continuity reasoning, troubleshooting, documentation, professional communication, preventive maintenance awareness, and workplace expectations through a final low-voltage project.

The week uses beginner-level electronics project patterns: source, wire path, switch or control point, resistor/protection, LED/buzzer/output, and return. These connect to the simple physical examples in the uploaded Forrest Mims beginner electronics PDF while staying within the course safety boundary.

Safety boundary: Final projects must be approved low-voltage DC projects. Students disconnect power before rewiring, continuity testing, resistance testing, or changing project scope.

Week 6 Skill Progression

  1. Day 1: Plan and approve a realistic low-voltage final project.
  2. Day 2: Build in stages, inspect before power, test safely, and record evidence.
  3. Day 3: Troubleshoot issues, verify operation, draft documentation, and add a preventive maintenance note.
  4. Day 4: Demonstrate project operation, explain evidence, answer questions, and complete rubric scoring.
  5. Day 5: Complete final quiz, certificate checklist, project submission, workplace expectations, and career reflection.

Recommended Final Project Materials

  • Approved low-voltage source or battery holder.
  • LEDs, buzzers, resistors, switches, pushbuttons, jumpers, breadboards, and terminal samples.
  • DMMs with working leads.
  • Student project trays or bags for storing work-in-progress.
  • Project plan forms, build/test records, repair ticket forms, rubric sheets, and final submission checklists.
  • Optional known-good example boards for students needing a visual reference.
