Week 6: Final Project, Documentation, and Career Pathways

Students demonstrate safe tool use, circuit understanding, continuity testing, troubleshooting, documentation, and professional communication through a final project.

Module Description

The final week is performance-based. Students choose or receive an approved final project, test it safely, document measurements, explain their work, and complete required certificate checkoffs.

Learning Outcomes

  • Complete a basic low-voltage final project using safe work habits.
  • Use a DMM and continuity testing to verify project function.
  • Document build steps, measurements, faults, corrections, and final test results.
  • Identify next-step pathways such as industrial maintenance, automation, robotics, telecommunications, or renewable energy technologies.

Required Lesson, Lab, and Assessments

  • Lesson 12: Final Project, Documentation, and Career Pathways
  • Lab 8: Final Project Lab
  • Week 6 Final Quiz
  • Final Project Rubric
  • Certificate Competency Checklist

Final Project Options

  • Wire and test an LED lighting circuit.
  • Build a continuity testing board.
  • Assemble a basic alarm circuit using a switch and low-voltage buzzer.
  • Troubleshoot and repair a training board fault.
