Student Reading: Learn This First

Day 4 is demonstration day. A demonstration is not just holding up a project and saying, “Look, it works!” It is a chance to prove that you understand what you built, how it works, how you tested it, and what you would do if it failed.

Your explanation should follow the same circuit story you have used all course: source, control, load, return. Point to each part. Say what it does. Then describe what should happen when the control changes state. If you can trace the project with your finger and your words, you are showing real understanding.

Evidence makes the demonstration stronger. Evidence might be a function test, a voltage measurement with units, a continuity result taken with power off, a visible inspection note, or a repair-ticket entry from Day 3. “It works because I said so” is not evidence. That line is for cartoon villains, not technicians.

It is okay if your project needed a correction. In fact, explaining a problem clearly can show strong technician thinking. Say the symptom, the evidence, the correction, and the verification. A project that had a fault and was fixed safely may teach more than a project that worked on the first try but has no documentation.

Professional communication matters. Speak clearly, admit uncertainty when needed, and use your notes. Technicians do not have to pretend they remember everything. They need to know how to find evidence, explain decisions, and stay inside safety boundaries.

Before your demo, practice this sentence: “My project uses __ as the source, __ as the control, __ as the load, and __ as the return; I verified it by __.”

Beginner Extra Help: Show, Point, Explain, Prove

Use four moves during the demonstration. Show the project operating. Point to the source, control, load, return, and protection. Explain the intended path. Prove the result with evidence from your documentation.

If you get nervous, return to the script. Purpose, source, control, path, load, return, safety, evidence, troubleshooting, verification, maintenance. That order is your safety rail.

Joke check: waving at the breadboard and saying “science happened” is not a technical explanation, even if it is technically true in a very broad sense.

Day 4 Purpose

Students demonstrate their final project and explain the safety habits, circuit path, measurements, troubleshooting steps, maintenance observations, and documentation used to complete it. The demonstration is both a technical check and a communication check. A technician must be able to make a system work and explain what was done.

Learning Targets

  • Demonstrate safe final project operation or clearly document unresolved fault status.
  • Explain the project using source-control-load-return vocabulary.
  • Show measurement, continuity, function-test, or inspection evidence.
  • Use documentation to support the demonstration.
  • Respond to instructor questions professionally.
  • Identify one improvement or next-step skill.

Student Demonstration Script

  1. Purpose: "My project is designed to..."
  2. Source: "The approved source is..."
  3. Control: "The output is controlled by..."
  4. Path: "When the circuit works, current follows this intended path..."
  5. Load/output: "The output is..."
  6. Return: "The return path is..."
  7. Safety: "Before applying power, I checked..."
  8. Evidence: "My test or measurement evidence is..."
  9. Troubleshooting: "One problem I found was... The evidence was... I corrected it by..."
  10. Verification: "After correction, the project..."
  11. Maintenance: "One maintenance or handoff note is..."

Instructor Follow-Up Questions

  • What would happen if the return path opened?
  • Where is current limited in your project?
  • What test gave you the best evidence?
  • What did you change after the first build?
  • How do you know the correction worked?
  • What would you inspect before another class used this project?
  • What would you improve if you built it again?
  • Which part of your project is most likely to fail or come loose?
  • What would be unsafe or out of bounds for this project?
