Student Reading: Learn This First

Compare expected operation to actual operation. The difference between those two statements is the troubleshooting problem.

Make the project safe before touching wiring. Disconnect power before resistance checks, continuity checks, or component changes.

Choose one section to inspect or test. Use the source-control-load-return model to decide whether to begin at the source, control, load, or return.

Collect evidence before correction. Evidence may be a visible wrong row, a loose connector, a voltage reading, or an open continuity path.

Revise one thing at a time. After the change, retest and document whether the symptom changed.

Finish with maintenance thinking. Check labels, wire strain, connector seating, storage, tool cleanup, and whether another student could understand the documentation.

Lesson Work

  1. Record expected and actual project operation.
  2. Use the troubleshooting process on one issue.
  3. Document evidence, correction, and verification.
  4. Complete a maintenance and storage check.
