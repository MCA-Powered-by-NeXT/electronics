Day 5 Purpose

Students complete final assessment items, verify required competencies, submit final documentation, and connect course skills to technician pathways. This day closes the course with evidence of what students can do: work safely, explain circuits, use basic tests, troubleshoot with evidence, document clearly, and communicate professionally.

Learning Targets

  • Complete the final quiz and corrections if assigned.
  • Review and verify required course competencies.
  • Submit final project documentation packet.
  • Complete any final project corrections or retry items.
  • Explain workplace expectations: safety, communication, tool care, documentation, and coachability.
  • Identify possible next-step career or training pathways.
  • Reflect on strongest technician habit and next growth goal.

Final Review Targets

  • Safety loop: stop, inspect, control power, ask when unsure, document.
  • Electrical model: source, path, load, return, control point.
  • Quantities: voltage, current, resistance, continuity, power in beginner terms.
  • Components: resistor, LED, switch, fuse, wire, connector, terminal, buzzer/lamp.
  • DMM setup: correct function, correct jacks, circuit state, units, controlled probes.
  • Continuity testing: power off, endpoints named, result interpreted.
  • Troubleshooting: symptom, expected operation, evidence, correction, verification.
  • Documentation: clear enough for another person to understand.

Final Quiz

  1. Explain the technician safety loop.
  2. Define source, path, load, return, and control point.
  3. Explain the difference between an open circuit and an unintended path.
  4. Name the correct safety habit before rewiring or continuity testing.
  5. Explain what a resistor does in an LED circuit.
  6. State two DMM setup checks before measuring assigned voltage.
  7. Explain why resistance and continuity testing are done with power off.
  8. Describe the eight-step troubleshooting sequence.
  9. Explain what belongs in a complete repair ticket.
  10. Describe how your final project was verified.
  11. Write one professional technician note using symptom, evidence, correction, and verification.
  12. Name three workplace expectations for a technician.
  13. Name one career area where these skills could be useful and explain why.
  14. Identify one skill you should practice next after this course.
