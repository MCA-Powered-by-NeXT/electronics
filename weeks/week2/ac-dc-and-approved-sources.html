Week 2 Support: AC/DC and Approved Sources

Supplemental classroom content for AC/DC concepts, batteries, and approved classroom DC sources.

Week 2Day 1Day 4

Purpose

This page adds the outline topics AC vs DC power, batteries, and approved power sources to the Week 2 sequence. Students identify source types and labels while keeping all student lab work limited to approved low-voltage DC training sources.

Learning Targets

  • Distinguish DC from AC in beginner-friendly language.
  • Identify common battery and approved DC training-source labels.
  • Explain why classroom labs use low-voltage DC sources.
  • Record source information before building or testing a circuit.

AC vs DC Overview

TypeBeginner MeaningCommon ExamplesCourse Use
DCDirect current; polarity stays consistent in a simple source such as a battery.AA battery, 9 V battery, USB trainer, approved low-voltage DC trainer.Used for student labs and final projects.
ACAlternating current; the source changes direction repeatedly.Building power and utility power.Identified conceptually only in this beginner course.

Power Source Inspection Checklist

  • [ ] Source is assigned or approved by instructor.
  • [ ] Source type identified.
  • [ ] Voltage rating recorded.
  • [ ] Polarity identified before connecting.
  • [ ] Leads/connectors are not damaged.
  • [ ] Source is disconnected before wiring changes.
