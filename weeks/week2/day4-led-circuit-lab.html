Week 2 Day 4: Battery Voltage and LED Circuit Lab

2-3 hour class block for measuring battery voltage and building a current-limited LED circuit.

Week 2Previous DayNext Day

Day 4 Purpose

Students apply the Week 2 concepts by inspecting parts, checking polarity, measuring battery voltage, building a simple LED circuit, and documenting the result.

Safety reminder: Disconnect power before changing wires. Use only instructor-approved low-voltage batteries or training sources.

Learning Targets

  • Measure battery voltage with instructor-approved DMM setup.
  • Identify LED polarity and resistor placement.
  • Build a current-limited LED circuit.
  • Document measurements and observations.

Tools and Parts

  • Battery or approved low-voltage source
  • Breadboard
  • LED
  • Current-limiting resistor
  • Jumper wires
  • DMM and leads
  • Notebook or lab sheet

Procedure

  1. Inspect the bench and parts before touching the circuit.
  2. Identify battery positive and negative.
  3. Measure and record battery voltage if directed by the instructor.
  4. Identify LED anode and cathode.
  5. Place the resistor in series with the LED.
  6. Trace source, path, load, and return before connecting power.
  7. Connect power and observe the LED.
  8. Disconnect power before changing any wiring.
  9. Document the final circuit and any corrections.
