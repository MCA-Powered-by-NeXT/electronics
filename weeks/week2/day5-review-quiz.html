Student Reading: Learn This First

Review the four quantities in circuit order. Source relates to voltage. Complete path relates to current. Resistor relates to resistance. Load relates to power and energy conversion.

Write each unit and what it measures. Volts measure voltage, amperes measure current, ohms measure resistance, and watts measure power.

Review resistor color code as a reading skill. Do not guess resistor values from memory or color impression. Read bands and write units.

Review Ohm's Law as a prediction tool. The formula helps estimate current or resistance, but it does not replace inspection.

Review the LED circuit as a complete example. Explain why the resistor matters, why LED polarity matters, and why the return matters.

Prepare for Week 3 by writing three measurement questions: one for voltage, one for resistance, and one for continuity. These questions will become DMM tasks.

Lesson Work

  1. Complete quantity and unit review.
  2. Decode a resistor and explain its purpose.
  3. Solve one Ohm's Law problem and interpret it.
  4. Complete the Week 2 quiz and corrections.
  5. Write Week 3 DMM readiness questions.
