Student Reading: Learn This First

Week 2 is about giving names and numbers to what happens in a circuit. Week 1 said a circuit needs a complete loop. Week 2 says that loop can be described with voltage, current, resistance, power, resistor value, and Ohm’s Law.

Review the quantities as a story. The source provides voltage. A complete path allows current. Resistance limits or controls current. The load uses electrical energy. Power describes how quickly energy is used or converted.

Review resistors as practical parts, not just color puzzles. A resistor value matters because it affects current. A current-limiting resistor is part of the safe design of an LED circuit.

Review Ohm’s Law as a prediction tool. A calculation can help you estimate what should happen, but it does not replace safe building. The breadboard can still be wrong even when the math is right. Math does not magically move a jumper wire into the correct row. Sadly, math refuses to clean the bench too.

Review documentation. A beginner note says “it lit up.” A stronger technician note says, “Battery measured __ V DC. Resistor value was __ Ω. LED lit after correcting missing return wire.” The second note gives evidence.

Fun fact: most technicians become faster by becoming more careful, not by rushing. Careful notes, correct units, and one-change-at-a-time troubleshooting prevent repeat mistakes. Slow is smooth; smooth becomes fast.

Before the quiz, complete this sentence: “I am ready for Week 3 meter work because I can identify __, record __, inspect __, and explain __.”

Day 5 Purpose

Students show that they can explain and apply Week 2 ideas before moving into formal DMM and continuity testing. The review is not just a quiz period; it is a chance to correct unit confusion, resistor errors, and unsafe lab habits before Week 3.

Learning Targets

  • Review voltage, current, resistance, power, and units.
  • Decode resistor values at an introductory level.
  • Use Ohm's Law for simple problems.
  • Explain how a current-limited LED circuit works.
  • Identify which Week 2 skill needs more practice before Week 3.

Week 2 Quiz

  1. Define voltage, current, and resistance in your own words.
  2. Match each unit to its quantity: V, A, Ω, W.
  3. Explain why an LED needs a current-limiting resistor.
  4. Decode this resistor: red-red-brown-gold.
  5. Solve: A 9 V source is connected through 300 Ω. What current is predicted?
  6. Name two safety checks before powering an LED circuit.
  7. Explain source, path, load, and return in your Week 2 LED circuit.
  8. What should you do if a part becomes hot?
  9. Why is a measurement without a unit incomplete?
  10. What should a technician document after correcting a circuit mistake?

Correction Prompts

  • If you missed a unit question, rewrite all four quantities with correct units.
  • If you missed a resistor question, decode two more sample resistors.
  • If you missed an Ohm's Law question, solve one new problem using the full setup routine.
  • If you missed a safety question, write the pre-power inspection checklist from memory.

Week 3 Readiness Checklist

  • [ ] I can keep units with measurements and calculations.
  • [ ] I can identify resistor purpose in an LED circuit.
  • [ ] I can explain why DMM setup matters before measuring.
  • [ ] I can disconnect power before changing wiring.
  • [ ] I can write a useful observation note, not just "it worked" or "it did not work."

Write three sentences: one about voltage, one about resistance, and one about why meter setup will matter in Week 3.
