Week 2 Day 1: Voltage, Current, and Resistance

2-3 hour class block introducing the main electrical quantities technicians use in basic DC circuits.

Week 2Previous WeekNext Day

Day 1 Purpose

Students connect the Week 1 circuit loop to the quantities that describe circuit behavior. Voltage, current, and resistance are introduced as practical technician words, not abstract math.

Learning Targets

  • Define voltage, current, resistance, and power in beginner language.
  • Identify common units: volts, amps, ohms, and watts.
  • Explain why a complete path is required before current can flow.
  • Connect voltage, current, and resistance to a simple LED circuit.

Key Vocabulary

TermUnitBeginner Meaning
VoltageVolts (V)The electrical push supplied by a battery or approved source.
CurrentAmps (A)The flow of electric charge through a complete path.
ResistanceOhms (Ω)Opposition to current flow.
PowerWatts (W)The rate at which electrical energy is used.

Main Concept

Voltage pushes, current flows, and resistance limits or opposes that flow. In a beginner LED circuit, the battery supplies voltage, current flows through the path, and a resistor helps limit current so the LED is protected.

Battery voltage -> current through path -> resistor limits current -> LED uses energy -> return path completes loop
