Day 3 Purpose

Students use Ohm's Law as a simple technician tool. The focus is practical: if voltage, current, and resistance are related, then changing one value affects the others.

Learning Targets

  • Use V = I x R for simple calculations.
  • Rearrange the formula with instructor support.
  • Explain current limiting in a simple LED circuit.
  • Show work with units.

Main Concept

V = I x R
Voltage = Current x Resistance

Ohm's Law helps technicians predict what may happen in a circuit. In this course, calculations stay simple and support safe low-voltage decisions.

Current Limiting

An LED can be damaged if too much current flows through it. A resistor placed in the intended path limits current and protects the LED. The resistor is not an optional decoration; it is part of the safe circuit design.
