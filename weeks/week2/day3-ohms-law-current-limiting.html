Student Reading: Learn This First

Voltage, current, and resistance are connected. Changing one affects what happens in the circuit.

Ohm's Law is commonly written as V = I × R. V is voltage in volts, I is current in amperes, and R is resistance in ohms.

If voltage stays the same and resistance increases, current decreases. If resistance stays the same and voltage increases, current increases.

This relationship explains current limiting. A resistor in series with an LED limits current through the LED. Without the correct resistor in the path, the LED may be too bright, fail, or behave unpredictably.

Use calculations as predictions. The calculation tells what should happen if the circuit is built correctly. It does not prove the circuit is wired correctly.

After each calculation, write an interpretation sentence: “This current estimate is reasonable because __” or “This value needs review because __.”

Check your work by identifying known values, choosing what you are solving for, calculating, and explaining the result in words.

Lesson Work

  1. Identify V, I, and R in sample problems.
  2. Solve simple Ohm's Law practice problems.
  3. Explain why a resistor is used with an LED.
  4. Write a prediction sentence before building or measuring.
