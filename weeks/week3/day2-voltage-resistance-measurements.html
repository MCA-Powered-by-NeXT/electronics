Week 3 Day 2: Voltage and Resistance Measurements

2-3 hour class block for approved DC voltage and power-off resistance measurement practice.

Week 3Previous DayNext Day

Day 2 Purpose

Students practice measurement as evidence. They set up the DMM, measure approved low-voltage sources and resistors, record units, and explain what the measurement means.

Learning Targets

  • Measure DC voltage on approved low-voltage sources.
  • Measure resistance only on disconnected components.
  • Record measurements with units and notes.
  • Compare expected and measured values.

Voltage Measurement Procedure

  1. Inspect the meter and leads.
  2. Set the meter for DC voltage.
  3. Place black lead in COM and red lead in the voltage jack.
  4. Touch probes to the approved battery or source terminals.
  5. Read the display and record the number with V.

Resistance Measurement Procedure

  1. Verify the component is disconnected from power.
  2. Set the meter for resistance.
  3. Touch probes to the resistor leads.
  4. Wait for a stable reading.
  5. Record the value with ohms.
