Week 3 Day 2: Voltage and Resistance Measurements

2-3 hour class block for approved DC voltage and power-off resistance measurement practice.

Week 3Previous DayNext Day

Day 2 Purpose

Students practice measurement as evidence. They set up the DMM, measure approved low-voltage sources and resistors, record units, and explain what the measurement means. Students should learn that a meter reading is not just a number; it is evidence that must be connected to the circuit condition.

Learning Targets

  • Measure DC voltage on approved low-voltage sources.
  • Measure resistance only on disconnected components.
  • Record measurements with units and notes.
  • Compare expected and measured values.
  • Explain the difference between powered voltage measurement and unpowered resistance measurement.

Voltage Measurement Procedure

  1. Inspect the meter and leads.
  2. Set the meter for DC voltage.
  3. Place black lead in COM and red lead in the voltage/resistance jack.
  4. Confirm the source is an approved low-voltage source.
  5. Touch probes to the source terminals with controlled probe tips.
  6. Read the display and record the number with V.
  7. Note polarity if the meter shows a negative sign.

Resistance Measurement Procedure

  1. Verify the component is disconnected from power.
  2. Remove or isolate the resistor if directed by the instructor.
  3. Set the meter for resistance.
  4. Touch probes to the resistor leads.
  5. Wait for a stable reading.
  6. Record the value with ohms.
  7. Compare measured value to the color-code value.

Common Mistakes

  • Measuring resistance while the component is still powered.
  • Using AC voltage setting for a DC battery.
  • Recording a reading without units.
  • Thinking a slightly different resistor reading is automatically a bad part.
  • Letting probe tips slip and touch each other.

Knowledge Check and Exit Ticket

1. Why is resistance measured with power off?
2. Which meter function is used for a battery?
3. Why should units be recorded?
4. What does expected vs measured mean?
Exit ticket: Explain one difference between voltage measurement and resistance measurement.
