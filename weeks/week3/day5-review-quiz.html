Week 3 Day 5: Review, Quiz, and Readiness Check

2-3 hour class block for DMM review, continuity review, quiz, corrections, and Week 4 readiness.

Week 3Previous DayWeek 4

Day 5 Purpose

Students confirm they understand meter setup, safe measurement habits, and continuity testing before using these skills with components and schematics.

Review Targets

  • DMM parts, jacks, dial, and leads.
  • DC voltage measurement on approved sources.
  • Resistance measurement with power off.
  • Continuity testing of wires, switches, and fuses.
  • Documentation with units.

Week 3 Quiz

  1. What should you check before using a DMM?
  2. Which jack does the black lead normally use?
  3. Why is current mode treated with extra caution?
  4. When is it safe to measure resistance?
  5. What does continuity mean?
  6. How should a closed switch test?
  7. Why are units important in measurement records?
  8. What should you do if a meter setup looks wrong?

Correction Prompts

  • Explain the difference between voltage, resistance, and continuity tests.
  • Write the safe setup for measuring a battery.
  • Write the safe setup for checking a fuse.
  • Explain why power-off testing matters.

Week 4 Readiness Checklist

  • [ ] I can inspect and set up a DMM before measuring.
  • [ ] I can record measurements with units.
  • [ ] I can test continuity only when power is off.
  • [ ] I can explain why schematic tracing and continuity testing go together.
