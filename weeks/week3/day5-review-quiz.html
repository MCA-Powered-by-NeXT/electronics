Student Reading: Learn This First

Sort questions before meter settings. Ask: What do I need to know? Source voltage? Resistor value? Whether a path is complete? Whether current mode is unsafe?

For voltage questions, choose DC voltage, approved powered low-voltage points, correct jacks, and controlled probes.

For resistance questions, choose resistance, power off, and assigned component or isolated part.

For continuity questions, choose continuity or low resistance, power off, and named endpoints.

For current-mode questions, stop and ask. Recognize current jacks and settings so you do not accidentally use them for voltage.

Write a review note: “The question is __, so the correct meter task is __. The circuit should be __. The result should be recorded with __.”

Lesson Work

  1. Sort scenarios into voltage, resistance, continuity, or current-mode stop rule.
  2. Rewrite weak measurement notes.
  3. Complete Week 3 quiz and corrections.
  4. Complete Week 4 readiness checklist.
