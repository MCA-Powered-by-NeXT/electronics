Student Reading: Learn This First

The DMM checkoff tests process, not luck. A correct-looking number is not enough if the setup was unsafe or unexplained.

Review the four station types: voltage, resistance, continuity, and current-mode awareness. Know what circuit state each station requires before touching probes.

At the voltage station, identify approved source, jacks, DC voltage setting, probe points, reading, unit, and meaning.

At the resistance station, verify power off, identify the resistor, use correct jacks and function, read the value, and compare to expected.

At the continuity station, name endpoints, verify power off, read beep/low resistance/open result, and explain what it means.

At the current-mode station, stop and explain why current mode is special-use. Recognition is the skill; independent current measurement is not the task.

Rehearse one complete checkoff explanation aloud before performing it.

Lesson Work

  1. Practice all checkoff stations before scoring.
  2. Complete DMM setup station.
  3. Complete voltage, resistance, and continuity tasks with notes.
  4. State current-mode stop rule.
  5. Complete retry work if any safety or documentation habit is missed.
