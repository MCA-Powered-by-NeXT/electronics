Day 4 Purpose

Students demonstrate that they can safely set up and use a DMM for beginner low-voltage tasks before moving into schematic and component work. This day is a practical skills day, not a lecture day. Students should practice, receive coaching, retry safely, and show consistent habits.

The checkoff evaluates process, not just final readings. A student who gets a reasonable number but used the wrong setup or cannot explain the circuit state needs coaching and retry.

Learning Targets

  • Select correct meter settings and jacks for DC voltage, resistance, and continuity.
  • Measure DC voltage on approved sources.
  • Measure resistance on disconnected or isolated components.
  • Perform continuity tests on unpowered parts or paths.
  • Document results with units, item tested, circuit state, and meaning.
  • Explain why each meter setup is safe for the task.
  • Recognize current-mode stop rules.

Retry Guidance

A retry should focus on the specific missed habit. For example, if a student forgot units, the retry should require three measurement notes with units. If a student chose the wrong jack, the retry should require setup explanation before any probe contact. If a student skips power-off confirmation, the retry must include power-state verbalization before every resistance/continuity task.
