Week 3 Day 4: DMM Practical Checkoff

2-3 hour class block for DMM skill practice, coaching, and practical signoff.

Day 4 Purpose

Students demonstrate that they can safely set up and use a DMM for beginner low-voltage tasks before moving into schematic and component work. This day is a practical skills day, not a lecture day. Students should practice, receive coaching, retry safely, and show consistent habits.

Learning Targets

  • Select correct meter settings and jacks.
  • Measure DC voltage on approved sources.
  • Measure resistance on disconnected components.
  • Perform continuity tests on unpowered parts.
  • Document results with units and short notes.
  • Explain why each meter setup is safe for the task.

Checkoff Stations

StationTaskRequired Student Statement
1Set up DMM for DC battery voltage."I am measuring DC voltage, so the circuit is powered and my leads are in COM and voltage."
2Measure resistor value."I am measuring resistance, so the component is disconnected from power."
3Check continuity of a wire or switch."I am checking whether this path is complete, and power is off."
4Record a measurement note."My note includes the reading, unit, and what the reading means."

Retry Guidance

A retry should focus on the specific missed habit. For example, if a student forgot units, the retry should require three measurement notes with units. If a student chose the wrong jack, the retry should require setup explanation before any probe contact.
