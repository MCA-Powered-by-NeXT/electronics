Week 3: Digital Multimeter, Measurement, and Continuity Testing

Five 2-3 hour class meetings. Students learn meter setup, safe low-voltage measurements, power-off continuity testing, current-mode awareness, DMM practical checkoff skills, and measurement documentation.

Week Three Purpose

Week Three teaches students how to use a digital multimeter as a technician tool. The emphasis is on setup, circuit state, correct jacks, correct settings, controlled probes, units, and documentation. Students learn that a meter reading is useful only when they can explain what was measured, how it was measured, and what the reading means.

This week uses beginner electronics examples inspired by the uploaded Forrest Mims PDF: batteries, resistors, switches, fuses, wires, LEDs, and simple direct-current paths. All student measurement activities stay inside the low-voltage classroom safety boundary.

DMM safety note: Students use approved low-voltage training circuits only. Continuity and resistance are measured only with power disconnected. Current mode is treated as a special-use awareness topic unless the instructor explicitly controls the setup.

Week 3 Skill Progression

  1. Day 1: Students identify DMM parts, jacks, dial settings, setup checks, probe control, and current-mode stop rules.
  2. Day 2: Students measure approved DC voltage and power-off resistance, then document readings with units and meaning.
  3. Day 3: Students use power-off continuity testing on wires, switches, fuses, connectors, and simple paths.
  4. Day 4: Students complete DMM practical checkoffs with coaching and retries.
  5. Day 5: Students review, quiz, correct misconceptions, and connect DMM skills to Week 4 components/schematics.

Recommended Materials

  • DMMs with working leads and clearly labeled jacks.
  • Approved low-voltage DC classroom sources or batteries.
  • Resistors for isolated resistance measurement.
  • Wires, switches, connectors, and fuses/fuse holders for power-off continuity testing.
  • Unpowered props for setup practice before measuring.
  • Known-good and intentionally open wire samples for continuity comparison.
  • Measurement record sheets, checkoff rubrics, and scenario cards.

Week Three Completion Checklist
