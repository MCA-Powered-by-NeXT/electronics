Week 3 Day 1: DMM Parts and Safety

2-3 hour class block introducing digital multimeter parts, setup checks, and safe measurement habits.

Day 1 Purpose

Students learn that a DMM is useful only when the dial, jacks, leads, range, circuit state, and probe placement match the measurement being attempted.

Learning Targets

  • Identify display, dial, jacks, probes, and common meter symbols.
  • Select the correct setup for DC voltage, resistance, and continuity practice.
  • Explain why current mode requires instructor approval.
  • Use a setup checklist before measuring.

DMM Setup Checklist

  • [ ] What am I measuring?
  • [ ] Is the circuit powered or unpowered as required?
  • [ ] Is the black lead in COM?
  • [ ] Is the red lead in the correct jack?
  • [ ] Is the dial on the correct function?
  • [ ] Are the probe tips controlled?
  • [ ] Did I record units?

DMM Current Warning

Current mode caution: Never place a meter set to current mode directly across a battery or voltage source. Current measurements require the correct jack, current range, fuse protection, circuit placement, and instructor approval.

Common Mistakes

  • Leaving the red lead in the current jack after a previous task.
  • Trying to measure resistance on a powered circuit.
  • Touching probe tips together while probing a live low-voltage source.
  • Recording numbers without units.
  • Changing wires before disconnecting power.

Knowledge Check

1. What jack does the black lead normally use?
2. Why must the meter function match the task?
3. Why is current mode treated with extra caution?
4. What should you check before touching probes to a circuit?
Exit ticket: Write the DMM setup checklist in your own words.
