Student Reading: Learn This First

A digital multimeter does not decide what to measure. The technician decides the question first. The meter gives useful evidence only when it is set up for that question.

Identify the parts of the meter in order: display, dial, jacks, leads, and probes. The display shows the reading. The dial chooses the function. The jacks and leads control how the meter connects to the circuit.

Use the normal lead positions for this course. Black lead goes in COM. Red lead goes in the voltage/resistance jack for voltage, resistance, and continuity practice. Current jacks are special-use and require instructor control.

Circuit state matters. Voltage checks use approved powered low-voltage sources or points. Resistance and continuity checks require power off. This is one of the most important DMM safety habits.

Say the setup before touching probes: “I am measuring __. The circuit should be __. My black lead is __. My red lead is __. My dial is set to __.”

Write measurements as complete notes: item measured, circuit state, meter setting, reading, unit, and meaning. This prevents number-only notes.

Lesson Work

  1. Label a DMM display, dial, jacks, leads, and probes.
  2. Practice setup for voltage, resistance, continuity, and current-mode stop rule.
  3. Complete safety scenarios about wrong jack, wrong dial, and wrong circuit state.
  4. Practice probe control on unpowered props before measuring.
  5. Exit ticket: write the DMM setup checklist from memory.

Safety Boundary

Use only assigned low-voltage training sources. Do not use current mode independently. Do not measure unknown circuits.
