Week 3 Support: DMM Current-Mode Awareness

Supplemental classroom content for the multimeter current topic in the outline.

Week 3Day 1Day 5

Purpose

The outline includes current as a multimeter topic. This support page adds that topic as current-mode awareness. Students learn why current mode is different, why incorrect setup can damage equipment, and why current-mode work requires instructor approval.

Student rule: Students do not independently use current mode. Current mode is taught as a recognition, setup, and safety-awareness topic unless the instructor explicitly controls a protected low-voltage demonstration.

Learning Targets

  • Explain why current measurement is different from voltage measurement.
  • Recognize the DMM current jack and current range as special-use settings.
  • Explain why current measurement requires a series path.
  • Describe the danger of using current mode across a source.
  • State when to stop and ask the instructor.

Voltage vs Current vs Resistance

MeasurementMeter PlacementStudent UseSafety Note
VoltageAcross two approved low-voltage points.Student practice with assigned sources.Correct jack, function, and probe control required.
CurrentIn series with the circuit path.Awareness / instructor-controlled demonstration only unless explicitly approved.Incorrect setup can damage the meter, fuse, circuit, or parts.
Resistance / continuityAcross an unpowered part or path.Student practice with power off.Never use on powered circuits.

Current-Mode Stop Rules

  • [ ] Stop if the red lead is in a current jack and you are not doing an approved current-mode task.
  • [ ] Stop if the meter is set to A, mA, or current mode and you cannot explain the circuit path.
  • [ ] Stop if you cannot explain where the meter would be placed in series.
  • [ ] Stop if the circuit source is not assigned or approved.
  • [ ] Ask the instructor before changing jacks, ranges, or circuit placement for current mode.
