Student Reading: Learn This First

Week 1 review connects the week together. Day 1 taught safe habits. Day 2 used simulation. Day 3 introduced the circuit model. Day 4 practiced inspection. Day 5 asks whether you can use all four ideas together.

Explain the circuit loop without looking at notes. Say source, path, load, and return, then point to those parts in a simple circuit.

Explain three path conditions. A closed circuit has the intended complete path. An open circuit has a break. A short circuit has an unintended low-resistance path. Draw or describe one example of each.

A circuit is not ready for work just because it is simple. It must also have an approved source, visible wiring, safe bench condition, and clear instructions.

Write one readiness note that combines safety and circuit reasoning. Example: “This LED circuit is not ready for power because the return wire is missing and the source state is unclear.”

Voltage, current, and resistance in Week 2 will make more sense because you already understand the complete path they describe.

Lesson Work

  1. Complete safety and circuit vocabulary review.
  2. Draw and label a complete circuit loop.
  3. Explain open, closed, and short circuits.
  4. Complete the Week 1 quiz and corrections.
  5. Complete a readiness checklist for Week 2.

Exit Ticket

What is one safety habit and one circuit habit you will carry into Week 2?
