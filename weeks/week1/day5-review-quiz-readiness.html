Week 1 Day 5: Review, Quiz, and Readiness Check

2-3 hour class block for Week 1 review, quiz completion, corrections, and preparation for Week 2 circuit work.

Day 5 Purpose

Students review the full Week 1 foundation: safety habits, Tinkercad simulation, source-path-load-return vocabulary, hazard recognition, and documentation. The goal is to confirm readiness for Week 2 physical low-voltage circuit work.

Learning Targets

  • Review safety vocabulary and circuit vocabulary.
  • Complete the Week 1 quiz.
  • Correct misunderstandings before Week 2 circuit work.
  • Explain readiness for measuring batteries and building simple LED circuits.

Review: Safety Vocabulary

TermWhat Students Should Be Able To Say
HazardA condition that could injure someone, damage equipment, or create unsafe work.
PPEPersonal protective equipment used to reduce risk, such as safety glasses.
De-energizedPower has been removed and the state has been verified before work begins.
DocumentationWritten evidence of what was observed, changed, measured, or verified.

Review: Circuit Vocabulary

TermWhat Students Should Be Able To Say
SourceThe battery or approved supply that provides electrical energy.
PathThe conductive route current can travel through.
LoadThe part that uses electrical energy to do work, such as an LED.
ReturnThe path back to the source that completes the loop.
Open circuitAn incomplete path that stops current.
Short circuitAn unintended low-resistance path that may bypass the load and create heat or damage.

Reteach and Correction Prompts

  • Why is "power off first" a better technician habit than "be careful while it is powered"?
  • What is the difference between a circuit that is off because it is open and a circuit that is unsafe because it is shorted?
  • How can simulation help a student find wiring mistakes before using physical parts?
