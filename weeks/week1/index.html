Week One Purpose

Week One sets the expectations for the entire Basic Electronics Technician course. Before students build physical circuits, measure voltage, or troubleshoot faults, they need a reliable safety routine, a simple circuit vocabulary, and a safe simulation space for testing ideas.

Safety boundary: Week One physical activities use disconnected training equipment or low-voltage battery-powered DC circuits only. Students do not work on wall outlets, open power supplies, vehicle electrical systems, charged capacitors, or unknown energized equipment.

How To Use This Week

Use the daily pages as the primary classroom sequence. Each day page contains the teaching notes, vocabulary, activities, checks, and student tasks needed for that 2-3 hour class meeting.

What Students Should Be Ready To Do

  • Approach a workstation with an inspection mindset before touching parts or tools.
  • Use PPE and explain why it matters during low-voltage training activities.
  • Recognize hazards such as clutter, damaged leads, missing PPE, unclear power state, exposed conductors, and liquids near the bench.
  • Use Tinkercad Circuits to place a battery, switch, resistor, LED, and wires in a simulated workspace.
  • Use the source-path-load-return model to describe a simple circuit.
  • Distinguish open circuits, closed circuits, and short circuits in beginner-friendly language.
  • Write short notes that describe what was observed and what action should be taken.
