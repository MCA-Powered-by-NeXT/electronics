Week 1: Electrical Safety and Technician Fundamentals

Build the safety habits, vocabulary, and technician mindset students will use for the entire course.

Module Description

Week 1 introduces students to the role of an entry-level electronics technician and establishes the safety culture for all later labs. Students learn how to inspect a workstation, identify hazards, select basic PPE, treat unknown circuits as potentially energized, and explain the four parts of a simple circuit: source, path, load, and return.

Course safety boundary: All Week 1 activities use disconnected training equipment or low-voltage battery-powered DC circuits only. Students must not work on wall outlets, open power supplies, vehicle electrical systems, charged capacitors, or unknown energized equipment.

Weekly Learning Outcomes

  • Describe the work habits and responsibilities of an entry-level electronics technician.
  • Identify common lab hazards, including damaged leads, clutter, exposed conductors, liquids, incorrect PPE, and unclear power state.
  • Explain PPE, ESD awareness, and lockout/tagout awareness at an introductory level.
  • Use the source-path-load-return model to describe a simple circuit.
  • Distinguish open circuits, closed circuits, and short circuits in beginner-friendly language.

Required Lessons and Lab

Lesson 1: Safety and Technician Fundamentals

Technician roles, safe work habits, PPE, hazard recognition, ESD awareness, and LOTO awareness.

Lesson 2: Introduction to Electricity

Source, path, load, return, open circuits, closed circuits, short circuits, and simple circuit reasoning.

Lab 1: Safety Inspection and PPE

Students inspect a staged workstation, identify hazards, choose PPE, document corrective actions, and get instructor signoff.

Week 1 Quiz and Review

Week 1 Quiz checks hazard recognition, PPE, safe work sequence, circuit vocabulary, and the source-path-load-return model.

Review prompt: Why is "power off first" a better technician habit than "be careful while it is powered"?
