Week 1: Electrical Safety and Technician Fundamentals

Students begin with safe work habits, technician expectations, PPE, hazard recognition, and the basic idea that every circuit needs a source, path, load, and return.

Module Description

This week establishes the safety culture for the entire course. Students identify common hazards, practice PPE selection, learn why unknown circuits are treated as energized, and connect basic electrical vocabulary to real technician tasks.

Safety boundary: All demonstrations are low-voltage training circuits. Do not use wall outlets, open power supplies, or unknown energized equipment.

Learning Outcomes

  • Describe entry-level electronics technician duties and workplace expectations.
  • Identify common lab hazards and appropriate PPE.
  • Explain why power is removed before wiring or continuity testing.
  • Recognize the source, path, load, and return in a simple circuit.

Required Lessons and Lab

  • Lesson 1: Safety and Technician Fundamentals
  • Lesson 2: Introduction to Electricity
  • Lab 1: Safety Inspection and PPE
