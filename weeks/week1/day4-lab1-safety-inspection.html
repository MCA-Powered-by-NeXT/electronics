Student Reading: Learn This First

Inspection is a real technical skill. The goal is to collect visible evidence before touching, powering, measuring, or changing anything.

Use an inspection order. First inspect yourself: PPE, attention, and readiness. Second inspect the source: approved, low-voltage, and controlled. Third inspect wires and tools: no damage, no exposed conductor, no wrong meter lead position. Fourth inspect the bench: no liquids, clutter, loose metal, or hidden hazards.

Staged props are not meant to trick you. They train your eyes. A cup near electronics, a damaged wire, a loose jumper, or a cluttered board is a condition that should trigger a technician response.

Do not simply circle hazards. Explain why each condition matters. A loose wire might create an open circuit. Damaged insulation might create accidental contact. Clutter might hide the actual circuit path.

The inspection table should include what was seen, why it matters, and what should happen next. Avoid one-word answers.

Choose one inspected condition and write a professional sentence: “I observed __. This matters because __. The safe next action is __.”

Lesson Work

  1. Inspect the staged bench without touching anything.
  2. Record at least six conditions or hazards.
  3. For each condition, write the risk and corrective action.
  4. Discuss which hazards affect personal safety, equipment safety, and measurement reliability.
  5. Get instructor signoff before touching any trainer equipment.

Exit Ticket

Write one inspection note that includes observation, meaning, and safe next action.
