Five 2-3 hour class meetings. Students connect physical components to schematic symbols, trace circuits, compare physical and drawn paths, and use series/parallel reasoning to prepare for troubleshooting.

Week Four Purpose

Week Four helps students move between the physical circuit on a bench and the schematic drawing technicians use to plan, build, inspect, and troubleshoot. Students learn that parts have physical features, schematic symbols have meanings, and every useful circuit must still be traceable from source to load to return.

This week uses beginner-friendly examples inspired by the uploaded Forrest Mims electronics PDF: simple components, wires, switches, resistors, LEDs, fuses, and basic series/parallel paths. The course language remains original and all student activities stay inside the low-voltage training boundary.

Safety boundary: All builds remain low-voltage DC training circuits. Students disconnect power before changing components, moving wires, using continuity, or handling disconnected parts.

Week 4 Skill Progression

  1. Day 1: Students identify components, polarity, wiring habits, and handling notes.
  2. Day 2: Students connect parts to symbols, labels, nodes, and complete schematic paths.
  3. Day 3: Students rotate through component, symbol, physical tracing, schematic tracing, and documentation stations.
  4. Day 4: Students compare series and parallel paths using prediction, observation, and fault reasoning.
  5. Day 5: Students complete tracing/continuity checkoffs, quiz, corrections, and Week 5 readiness work.

Recommended Materials

  • Component trays with resistors, LEDs, diodes, switches, fuses or fuse holders, capacitors for visual ID only, wires, connectors, and terminals.
  • Disconnected training boards and low-voltage LED/switch/resistor circuits.
  • Symbol cards and physical part cards.
  • Simple schematics for source-switch-resistor-LED-return circuits.
  • Series and parallel trainer boards or instructor demo circuits.
  • Colored pencils or highlighters for tracing source, control, load, and return.
  • DMMs for power-off continuity checkoff only when assigned.

Week Four Completion Checklist

  • [ ] Day 1: Identify common components, purpose, polarity/handling notes, and wiring habits.
  • [ ] Day 2: Trace a simple schematic from source to return and identify labels/nodes.
  • [ ] Day 3: Match physical components to schematic symbols and write a technician note.
  • [ ] Day 4: Compare series and parallel paths and predict fault effects.
  • [ ] Day 5: Complete continuity/tracing checkoff, Week 4 quiz, corrections, and Week 5 readiness checklist.
