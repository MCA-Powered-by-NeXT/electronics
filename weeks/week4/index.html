Module Description

This week connects physical parts to the symbols and diagrams technicians use at work. Students practice reading simple schematics and then build and measure matching circuits.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify resistors, LEDs, diodes, capacitors, switches, fuses, wires, connectors, and terminals.
  • Recognize common schematic symbols and reference labels.
  • Trace a simple circuit from source to load to return.
  • Explain how voltage and current behave differently in series and parallel circuits.

Required Lessons, Labs, and Checkoff

  • Lesson 7: Common Components and Wiring Basics
  • Lesson 8: Reading Basic Schematics
  • Lesson 9: Series and Parallel Circuits
  • Lab 5: Component Identification and Schematics
  • Lab 6: Series and Parallel Circuits
  • Continuity Practical Checkoff

Quiz and Review

Week 4 Quiz includes component identification, schematic symbols, polarity, series/parallel behavior, and continuity interpretation.

Enrichment: Diode, capacitor, and RC timing concepts can be used as demonstrations without advanced math.
