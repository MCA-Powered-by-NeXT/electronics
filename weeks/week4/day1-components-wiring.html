Student Reading: Learn This First

Components are parts with jobs. A technician does not just recognize shapes; a technician explains what each part does in the circuit.

Resistors limit current or create a known resistance in a path. Connect resistor purpose to the LED circuits built earlier.

LEDs and diodes are direction-sensitive. The way they are placed matters because they conduct or operate as intended only with correct orientation in a simple DC circuit.

Switches, fuses, wires, connectors, and terminals are path parts. A switch controls whether the path is open or closed. A fuse protects by opening under certain fault conditions. Wires and terminals create connection points.

Inspect component clues: labels, stripes, bands, lead length, flat sides, plus/minus marks, loose terminals, and damaged wire.

Write a component note: part name, purpose, polarity or handling note, where it appears in the path, and one possible fault.

Lesson Work

  1. Identify resistors, LEDs, diodes, switches, fuses, wires, connectors, terminals, buzzers, and lamps.
  2. Record purpose and polarity/handling notes.
  3. Sort components by job in the circuit.
  4. Inspect wiring samples for loose, damaged, or unclear connections.
