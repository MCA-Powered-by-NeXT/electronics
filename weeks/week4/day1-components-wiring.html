Week 4 Day 1: Components and Wiring Basics

2-3 hour class block introducing common components and physical wiring habits.

Week 4Previous WeekNext Day

Day 1 Purpose

Students identify common parts and describe how each part appears in beginner circuits. The focus is recognizing the part, knowing whether polarity matters, and handling parts carefully. Students also learn that good wiring is part of communication: another technician should be able to follow the circuit path.

Learning Targets

  • Identify resistors, LEDs, diodes, capacitors, switches, fuses, wires, connectors, and terminals.
  • Explain which parts are polarized.
  • Use careful wiring habits that make paths easier to trace.
  • Document component observations.
  • Explain how workmanship affects future troubleshooting.

Wiring Habits

  • Keep source wires and return wires visually consistent when possible.
  • Avoid long loops that hide the actual path.
  • Do not force component leads into a breadboard.
  • Keep exposed metal from touching unintended points.
  • Route wires so the circuit can be inspected and explained.

Common Mistakes

  • Calling every small part a resistor.
  • Ignoring LED or diode polarity.
  • Letting wire color replace actual path tracing.
  • Building a circuit that works but cannot be inspected.

Knowledge Check and Exit Ticket

1. Which components in today's tray may be polarized?
2. Why does an LED need a resistor?
3. What does a switch do?
4. Why should wiring be neat?
Exit ticket: Name one component, its purpose, and one handling note.
