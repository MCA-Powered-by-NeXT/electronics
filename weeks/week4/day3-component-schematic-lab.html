Week 4 Day 3: Component ID and Schematic Lab

2-3 hour class block matching real parts to symbols and documenting simple circuit paths.

Week 4Previous DayNext Day

Day 3 Purpose

Students practice moving between bench parts and schematic representations. They identify components, match symbols, trace physical and drawn paths, and explain how the drawing represents the circuit's electrical connections.

Safety boundary: Use disconnected parts, instructor-approved training boards, or low-voltage circuits only. Disconnect power before handling or changing any wiring.

Learning Targets

  • Identify components from a tray or training board.
  • Match components to schematic symbols and labels.
  • Trace a simple physical and schematic path.
  • Explain one difference between physical layout and electrical connection.
  • Record lab observations clearly enough for another technician to understand.

Technician Prompt

Choose one circuit card or training board. Trace from source to load to return. Then explain how the schematic shows the same electrical connections even if the drawing is arranged differently than the physical board.

Technician note:

Common Mistakes

  • Matching a symbol by shape but forgetting the part's purpose.
  • Tracing the physical wire but not connecting it to the schematic line.
  • Skipping polarity notes for LEDs and diodes.
  • Writing a vague note such as "circuit has parts" instead of naming parts and path.

Reflection and Exit Ticket

What component was easiest to identify?
What symbol was easiest to confuse?
How does a schematic help a technician troubleshoot?
Exit ticket: Pick one part and write its physical name, symbol label, and circuit purpose.
