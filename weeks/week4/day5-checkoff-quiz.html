Student Reading: Learn This First

Review the four skills of the week: identify components, match symbols, trace paths, and reason about series/parallel circuits.

Identify a component and explain its job. A correct answer includes purpose, polarity or handling note, and possible fault.

Match a part to its schematic symbol and reference label. The goal is communication, not just matching.

Trace a source-to-return path on a schematic and then on the physical trainer. The two paths should describe the same electrical relationship.

Decide whether a section is series or parallel by counting paths, not by guessing from appearance.

Write a readiness sentence: “I can troubleshoot this circuit because I know the intended path, the component jobs, and what would happen if a section opened.”

Lesson Work

  1. Complete component identification checkoff.
  2. Complete schematic-symbol matching.
  3. Trace a source-to-return path.
  4. Explain one series and one parallel fault symptom.
  5. Complete quiz and corrections.
