2-3 hour class block for schematic review, continuity checkoff, quiz, and Week 5 readiness.

Day 5 Purpose

Students demonstrate that they can identify parts, read simple symbols, trace paths, compare series and parallel circuits, and use continuity reasoning before moving into troubleshooting.

Week 4 Quiz

  1. Name four common electronic components.
  2. What does a resistor do?
  3. What does a schematic show?
  4. What does R1 mean on a schematic?
  5. Explain series in your own words.
  6. Explain parallel in your own words.
  7. Why is continuity testing useful for schematic tracing?
  8. Why should power be disconnected before checking continuity?
  9. Describe one difference between physical layout and schematic connection.
  10. What happens when one load opens in a simple series path?

Correction Prompts

  • Redraw one schematic path and label source, load, and return.
  • Match five physical parts to their symbols.
  • Explain one series and one parallel example.
  • Write the correct continuity testing safety rule from memory.

Week 5 Readiness Checklist

  • [ ] I can identify common parts and symbols.
  • [ ] I can trace a path from source to return.
  • [ ] I can compare series and parallel paths.
  • [ ] I can use continuity reasoning to locate opens.
  • [ ] I can write observations that help with troubleshooting.
