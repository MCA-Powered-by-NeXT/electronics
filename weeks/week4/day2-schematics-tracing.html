Student Reading: Learn This First

A schematic is an electrical map. It is not a picture of the physical board. It shows how points connect, not where every part sits on the table.

Symbols are vocabulary. A resistor symbol means resistor. A switch symbol means a control contact. An LED symbol shows a light-emitting diode. A source symbol represents the battery or approved source.

Reference labels identify individual parts. R1 means resistor number one. SW1 means switch number one. LED1 names a specific LED.

A node is a shared electrical connection point. A node may appear as a line or dot on paper, a breadboard row in the lab, or a labeled terminal on a trainer.

Trace in order: source, control, protection or resistor, load, return. Do not stop at the load; the return completes the circuit.

Write a tracing sentence: “Starting at __, the path goes through __, then __, then __, and returns to __.”

Lesson Work

  1. Match component symbols to physical parts.
  2. Identify reference labels and nodes.
  3. Trace simple schematics from source to return.
  4. Compare a physical trainer path to the schematic path.
