Course Description

This introductory course provides foundational knowledge and practical skills needed to safely work with basic electrical and electronic systems. Students learn electrical safety, Tinkercad circuit simulation, proper digital multimeter use, continuity testing, component identification, basic circuit construction, simple schematic reading, troubleshooting, and workplace documentation.

Duration: 6 weeks, 5 class days per week, 2-3 hours per day. Delivery: Classroom instruction plus hands-on low-voltage lab. Audience: adult beginners, high school CTE students, workforce development participants, and high school graduates.

Course Goals

  • Practice safe low-voltage electronics work habits.
  • Use Tinkercad to simulate beginner circuits before physical builds.
  • Use a DMM correctly for voltage, resistance, and continuity.
  • Build simple battery-powered DC circuits using LEDs, resistors, switches, fuses, and jumper wires.
  • Read and draw basic schematic representations.
  • Troubleshoot open circuits, shorts, wrong polarity, poor connections, and failed parts.
  • Document measurements, repairs, and final project results.

Assessment and Completion

Suggested completion model: weekly quizzes 30%, labs and worksheets 30%, practical skill checkoffs 20%, final project 20%. Safety-critical behaviors are required; a student who works unsafely must stop, receive coaching, and retry the task before continuing.

  • Students must complete all required labs or approved make-up activities.
  • Students must pass DMM, continuity, and troubleshooting checkoffs.
  • Students must complete a final project and demonstrate safe operation.
  • Student quiz pages do not include answers; answer keys belong in the instructor section only.

Safety Expectations

No mains work: Students do not measure wall outlets or unknown high-energy systems. All labs use low-voltage battery-powered DC circuits unless an instructor provides a specifically designed training source.

Students will wear required PPE, keep benches clear, disconnect power before rewiring, verify meter setup before measuring, and treat unknown circuits as potentially energized until verified safe.
