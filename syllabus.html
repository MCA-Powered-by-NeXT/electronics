Course Description

This introductory course provides foundational knowledge and practical skills needed to safely work with basic electrical and electronic systems. Students learn safety, DMM use, continuity testing, component identification, circuit construction, and troubleshooting.

Duration and Audience

6 weeks, 60-90 hours. Designed for beginners, workforce development participants, high school graduates, and adult learners.

Weekly Modules

  1. Electrical Safety and Technician Fundamentals
  2. Voltage, Current, Resistance, and Basic Circuits
  3. Digital Multimeter and Continuity Testing
  4. Components, Wiring, and Schematics
  5. Circuit Building and Applied Control
  6. Troubleshooting and Final Project

Assessment

Weekly quizzes, lab checkoffs, practical assessments, and a final project demonstration.
