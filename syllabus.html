Course Description

This introductory course provides foundational knowledge and practical skills needed to safely work with basic electrical and electronic systems. Students learn electrical safety, technician fundamentals, AC/DC source concepts, voltage/current/resistance, Ohm's Law, approved classroom source identification, proper digital multimeter use, continuity testing, component identification, wiring and connector inspection, basic circuit construction, simple schematic reading, troubleshooting, preventive maintenance awareness, workplace expectations, and documentation.

Duration: 6 weeks, 5 class days per week, 2-3 hours per day. Delivery: Classroom instruction plus hands-on low-voltage lab. Audience: adult beginners, high school CTE students, workforce development participants, and high school graduates.

Course Goals

  • Practice safe low-voltage electronics work habits.
  • Use Tinkercad to simulate beginner circuits before physical builds.
  • Identify classroom source types and use assigned DC training sources only.
  • Use a DMM correctly for approved voltage, resistance, continuity, and current-mode awareness tasks.
  • Build simple battery-powered DC circuits using LEDs, resistors, switches, fuses, and jumper wires.
  • Identify wires, connectors, terminals, and common components.
  • Read and draw basic schematic representations.
  • Troubleshoot open circuits, polarity errors, poor connections, wrong-value parts, and training-board faults.
  • Document measurements, repairs, preventive-maintenance observations, and final project results.
  • Explain workplace expectations and entry-level technician habits.

Assessment and Completion

Suggested completion model: weekly quizzes 30%, labs and worksheets 30%, practical skill checkoffs 20%, final project 20%. Safety-critical behaviors are required; a student who works unsafely must stop, receive coaching, and retry the task before continuing.

  • Students must complete all required labs or approved make-up activities.
  • Students must pass DMM, continuity, and troubleshooting checkoffs.
  • Students must complete a final project and demonstrate safe operation.
  • Students must submit technician-style documentation for labs, repair tickets, and the final project.
  • Student quiz pages do not include answers; answer keys belong in the instructor section only.

Safety Expectations

Students work only with assigned low-voltage classroom training sources. Non-training building power and unknown energized equipment are outside the student lab boundary.

Students will wear required PPE, keep benches clear, disconnect power before rewiring, verify meter setup before measuring, and treat unknown circuits as potentially energized until verified safe by the instructor.
