Instructional Designer: Mike Russell Systems Architect | Duration: 2 Weeks Intensive

Course Level: Introductory (No Prerequisites)
Path: Electronics Technology AAS / Repair Certification
Total Hours: 40 Hours (Lab + Theory)

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive foundation in fundamental electronic principles. Students progress from basic electrical safety to constructing and troubleshooting active circuits. We emphasize hands-on experience with professional diagnostic tools to prepare you for entry-level repair roles or further academic study.

Learning Outcomes

  • Implement industry-standard Electrical Safety and ESD protocols.
  • Calculate Voltage, Current, Resistance, and Power using Ohm’s/Watt’s Law.
  • Operate a Digital Multimeter (DMM) for component testing.
  • Interpret Schematic Diagrams and build them on breadboards.
  • Perform high-quality Soldering and Rework.
  • Execute a Systematic Troubleshooting process to isolate circuit faults.

Safety Expectations

CRITICAL: 1. Safety glasses are required at all times during soldering.
2. ESD straps must be used when handling semiconductors.
3. No work on high-voltage "Mains" circuitry is permitted.

Capstone Project

Students will be provided with a non-functional electronic device. To pass, you must verify the symptom, trace the circuit using a schematic, identify the fault (e.g., a blown diode or cold solder joint), and perform a successful repair with documented verification.
