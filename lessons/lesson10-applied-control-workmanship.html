Lesson 10: Applied Control and Workmanship

Estimated time: 2-3 hours. Connect switches, simple control ideas, and workmanship inspection.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how switches control a circuit path.
  • Describe a transistor as an electronic switch at an introductory level.
  • Explain why pull-up or pull-down resistors give inputs a known state.
  • Identify basic workmanship problems in prepared training examples.

Key Vocabulary

Control: a part of the circuit that decides when the load operates. Normally open: switch is open until pressed or actuated. Normally closed: switch is closed until pressed or actuated. Transistor switch: a small signal controls a larger load path. Workmanship: the physical quality of wiring, joints, and layout.

Main Concept

Technicians often troubleshoot control paths, not just loads. A load may be good, but it will not operate if the switch path, input state, connector, or transistor driver is wrong. Good workmanship makes faults less likely and easier to find.

Control idea: Button/sensor -> control device -> load turns on Bad workmanship clues: loose wire, exposed strand, poor seating, cold joint, bridge, wrong polarity
Beginner scope: Transistors and logic are introduced as practical control examples, not as a deep semiconductor or digital design course.

Technician Tips

  • Check the load and the control path separately.
  • Inspect before measuring. A loose wire or reversed LED may be visible.
  • For a button circuit, ask whether the input has a defined state when the button is not pressed.
  • Keep wiring readable; messy wiring hides mistakes.

Common Mistakes

  • Replacing a load before checking whether it receives power.
  • Leaving a digital input floating and expecting a stable reading.
  • Assuming a solder joint is good because it looks shiny in only one spot.
  • Ignoring connector seating and wire strain.

Knowledge Check

1. What is the difference between a normally open and normally closed switch?
2. Why might a circuit use a transistor instead of a mechanical switch?
3. Name two workmanship problems that can create intermittent faults.
