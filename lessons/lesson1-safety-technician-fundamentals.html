Lesson 1: Safety and Technician Fundamentals

Estimated time: 2-3 hours. Learn the technician mindset before touching tools or circuits.

Learning Outcomes

  • Describe what entry-level electronics technicians do.
  • Identify common lab hazards and safe responses.
  • Select appropriate PPE for beginner electronics labs.
  • Explain basic lockout/tagout awareness at a beginner level.
  • Describe why unknown circuits are treated as potentially energized.

Key Vocabulary

Hazard: anything that can cause harm. PPE: personal protective equipment. LOTO: lockout/tagout, a workplace process for controlling hazardous energy. ESD: electrostatic discharge. De-energized: power has been removed and verified safe.

Main Concept

A technician's first job is not to make the circuit work. It is to make the work safe, controlled, and documented. Beginner electronics work should use low-voltage battery-powered DC circuits. Even then, careless wiring can damage parts, heat wires, or create confusing faults.

Safe work flow: Inspect area -> Identify hazards -> Put on PPE -> Remove power -> Build or test -> Verify -> Document
Safety boundary: Do not work on wall outlets, mains-powered devices, open power supplies, vehicle electrical systems, charged capacitors, or unknown energized equipment in this course.

Technician Tips

  • Keep your bench clear enough that tools and leads do not accidentally bridge circuit points.
  • Use red wires for positive and black wires for ground when possible, but always verify with the circuit, not color alone.
  • Stop immediately if you see smoke, smell hot plastic, hear buzzing from an unexpected source, or feel a part heating quickly.
  • Ask before energizing a circuit you did not build or inspect.

Common Mistakes

  • Changing wiring while power is still connected.
  • Assuming a disconnected-looking wire is safe without testing or instructor confirmation.
  • Using a meter without checking the dial and lead jacks first.
  • Treating PPE as optional because the circuit is small.

Knowledge Check

1. List three hazards you might find at an electronics workstation.
2. Why should a circuit be powered off before rewiring?
3. What does LOTO awareness mean for a beginner, even if this course does not certify full LOTO practice?
