Lesson 2: Introduction to Electricity

Estimated time: 2-3 hours. Understand the basic parts every working circuit must have.

Previous LessonWeek 1Next LessonRelated LabWeek 1 Quiz

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify the source, path, load, and return in a simple circuit.
  • Explain open and closed circuits in beginner-friendly language.
  • Describe the difference between a normal load path and a short circuit.
  • Recognize why electricity requires a complete path.

Key Vocabulary

Source: battery or supply that provides electrical energy. Path: conductive route for current. Load: part that uses energy, such as an LED or buzzer. Return: path back to the source. Open: broken path. Short: unintended low-resistance path.

Main Concept

A circuit works only when there is a complete path from the source, through the load, and back to the source. If the path is open, current cannot flow. If current bypasses the load through an unintended low-resistance path, the circuit may overheat or damage the source.

Simple LED circuit: + Battery -> Resistor -> LED -> Battery - Open circuit: a break anywhere stops current. Short circuit: current bypasses the intended load.
Safe habit: Disconnect the battery before moving wires. Many beginner faults happen during quick changes.

Technician Tips

  • When a circuit is dead, first ask: do I have source voltage, a complete path, and a working load?
  • When something gets hot, suspect a short, wrong resistor value, or reversed part.
  • Draw a quick line from positive to negative before building; if you cannot trace the path on paper, the breadboard will be harder.

Common Mistakes

  • Thinking ground is a magic drain instead of a return/reference point.
  • Connecting an LED directly to a battery without a current-limiting resistor.
  • Using breadboard rows incorrectly and creating an accidental open circuit.

Knowledge Check

1. What four parts does every simple working circuit need?
2. What is the difference between an open circuit and a short circuit?
3. In the diagram, which part is the load?
