Lesson 7: Common Components and Wiring Basics

Estimated time: 2-3 hours. Identify the parts that appear in beginner electrical and electronics work.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify common components by appearance and function.
  • Explain basic resistor, LED, switch, fuse, and wire roles.
  • Recognize polarity-sensitive parts.
  • Inspect simple connectors and terminals for workmanship issues.

Key Vocabulary

Resistor: limits current. LED: light-emitting diode, polarity-sensitive. Switch: opens or closes a path. Fuse: opens during excessive current. Connector: removable electrical connection. Terminal: connection point for a wire or component lead.

Main Concept

Technicians identify parts by both appearance and role. A resistor is not just a striped cylinder; it is a current limiter or signal-setting part. A fuse is not just a wire in a tube; it is a safety device and must be replaced only with the correct rating.

Common component roles: Battery = source Fuse = protection Switch = control Resistor = current limit LED/Buzzer = load Wire/connector = path
Fuse rule: Never bypass a fuse as a repair. A blown fuse is evidence that must be investigated.

Technician Tips

  • Use resistor color code for a first estimate, then verify with the DMM when possible.
  • LEDs usually have a long lead for positive and a flat side or short lead for negative.
  • Pull gently on connectors only when appropriate; loose connectors create intermittent faults.
  • Inspect insulation for cuts, melted areas, or exposed conductor.

Common Mistakes

  • Assuming wire color always proves function.
  • Installing LEDs backwards and assuming the LED is dead.
  • Using a larger fuse because the correct one keeps opening.
  • Leaving component leads so long that they can short to other points.

Knowledge Check

1. What is the job of a resistor in an LED circuit?
2. Why is a fuse considered a protection device?
3. Name two signs of a bad connector or wire termination.
