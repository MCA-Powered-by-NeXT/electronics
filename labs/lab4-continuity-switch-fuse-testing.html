Lab 4: Continuity, Switch, and Fuse Testing

Week 3 required lab. Use power-off continuity testing to verify circuit integrity.

Week 3Lesson 6Week 3 QuizContinuity Checkoff

Safety Warning

Power-off only: Continuity and resistance testing must be performed only on disconnected, de-energized circuits and components.

Purpose and Skills Practiced

  • Verify meter continuity function.
  • Test wires for open paths.
  • Identify normally open and normally closed switch behavior.
  • Test fuses and record pass/fail results.

Tools and Parts

  • DMM with continuity mode
  • Wire samples
  • Momentary switch and toggle switch
  • Good fuse and intentionally open training fuse
  • Worksheet or notebook

Setup Diagram

Continuity test:
Probe A -> part under test -> Probe B

Good wire/fuse: beep or low ohms
Open wire/fuse: no beep or OL
Switch: result changes when actuated

Procedure

  1. Confirm all parts are disconnected from power.
  2. Select continuity mode and touch probes together to verify beep.
  3. Test each wire sample end-to-end.
  4. Test each switch in both positions or pressed/released states.
  5. Test each fuse and classify it as good or open.
  6. Record all results and explain one unexpected reading.

Cleanup and Reset

  1. Sort good and open training parts into labeled containers.
  2. Return switches and wire samples.
  3. Turn off the DMM.
  4. Report any confusing or damaged samples.
