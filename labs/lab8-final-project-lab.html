Lab 8: Final Project Lab

Week 6 required lab. Complete and demonstrate an approved final project.

Week 6Lesson 12Final QuizFinal RubricCertificate Checklist

Safety Warning

Approved projects only: Final projects must use low-voltage battery-powered DC circuits. No wall outlets, unknown devices, vehicle systems, or open power supplies are allowed.

Purpose and Skills Practiced

  • Plan, build, test, and document a simple electronics project.
  • Use a DMM and continuity testing safely.
  • Verify final operation using acceptance criteria.
  • Explain the project to an instructor or peer.

Project Options

  • LED lighting circuit with switch and current limiting.
  • Continuity testing board.
  • Low-voltage alarm circuit using a switch and buzzer.
  • Training board troubleshooting and repair demonstration.

Tools and Parts

  • DMM
  • Approved low-voltage battery source
  • Breadboard or training board
  • Project-specific parts approved by instructor
  • Notebook, schematic, or wiring plan

Setup Diagram

Final project workflow:
Plan -> Inspect -> Build -> Measure -> Troubleshoot -> Final test -> Document -> Demonstrate

Procedure

  1. Select an approved project option.
  2. Create a simple wiring plan or schematic.
  3. List required parts and safety checks.
  4. Build with power disconnected.
  5. Inspect the build and ask for instructor approval before powering.
  6. Measure required test points and record values.
  7. Troubleshoot and correct any faults.
  8. Demonstrate final operation and submit documentation.
