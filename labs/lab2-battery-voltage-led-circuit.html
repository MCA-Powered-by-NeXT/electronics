Lab 2: Battery Voltage and Simple LED Circuit

Week 2 required lab. Measure a battery and build a current-limited LED circuit.

Week 2Lesson 3Lesson 4Week 2 Quiz

Safety Warning

Low-voltage DC only: Use only instructor-provided batteries or approved low-voltage DC supplies. Do not measure wall outlets or unknown sources.

Purpose and Skills Practiced

  • Measure battery voltage with the DMM in DC voltage mode.
  • Identify battery polarity.
  • Build an LED circuit with a current-limiting resistor.
  • Record expected and actual observations.

Tools and Parts

  • DMM with leads
  • 3 V battery pack or 9 V battery
  • Breadboard
  • LED
  • Current-limiting resistor such as 330 ohm or 470 ohm for 9 V, as directed by instructor
  • Jumper wires

Setup Diagram

Battery + -> resistor -> LED anode
LED cathode -> Battery -

Long LED leg = anode (+)
Short LED leg / flat side = cathode (-)

Procedure

  1. Inspect your DMM leads and select DC voltage mode.
  2. Measure the battery voltage across the positive and negative terminals.
  3. Disconnect power before building the circuit.
  4. Place the resistor and LED on the breadboard according to the diagram.
  5. Check LED polarity and confirm there is no direct short from positive to negative.
  6. Connect the battery and observe the LED.
  7. Disconnect power and record results.
