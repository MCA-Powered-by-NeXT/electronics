Lab 1: Safety Inspection and PPE

Week 1 required lab. Practice the inspection routine students will use before every build, measurement, and troubleshooting activity.

Week 1 Lesson 1 Lesson 2 Week 1 Quiz

Prominent Safety Warning

Disconnected training equipment only: This lab uses instructor-staged, disconnected training equipment and simulated hazards. Do not touch wall outlets, open power supplies, vehicle systems, charged capacitors, or unknown energized equipment. If you are unsure whether something is powered, stop and ask the instructor.

Purpose and Skills Practiced

This lab turns safety language into visible action. Students will inspect before touching, identify hazards, explain why each hazard matters, and write practical corrective actions.

  • Inspect a workstation before starting technical work.
  • Identify hazards that could injure people, damage tools, damage components, or create false measurements.
  • Select appropriate PPE for beginner electronics labs.
  • Explain what corrective action should be taken for each hazard.
  • Practice documenting observations like a technician.

Tools and Parts

  • Safety glasses
  • Disconnected breadboard or training board
  • Disconnected battery holder or battery sample
  • Jumper wires and sample components
  • DMM with leads, used as an inspection prop only unless directed
  • Instructor-staged hazard cards or prepared workstation
  • Notebook or printed lab sheet
Instructor setup note: Use only safe props. Do not create real energized hazards for students to discover.

Before You Begin

  • [ ] Put on safety glasses.
  • [ ] Keep hands off the training board until the first visual inspection is complete.
  • [ ] Confirm the station is part of this lab and has been prepared by the instructor.
  • [ ] Prepare your notebook or worksheet for observations.

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Stand back and scan the whole bench before touching anything.
  2. Confirm that the training board is disconnected from all power sources.
  3. Identify the source area, tool area, parts tray, and work area.
  4. Inspect wires and leads for damaged insulation, exposed metal, loose connectors, or sharp ends.
  5. Inspect the work area for clutter, liquids, metal objects, and trip or snag hazards.
  6. Inspect the DMM as a prop: note whether leads appear undamaged and whether lead placement would match a safe voltage/resistance task.
  7. Record at least five observations in the inspection table.
  8. For every unsafe item, write a corrective action that someone could actually perform.
  9. Choose one hazard and explain why it matters to a partner.
  10. Ask the instructor to review your findings.
  11. Reset the station only after instructor review.

Troubleshooting and Discussion Questions

  1. Why should a disconnected power source still be part of the safety inspection?
  2. Which hazard would make you stop work immediately?
  3. What is one hazard that could damage components but not obviously injure a person?
  4. How could clutter create a false measurement or accidental short circuit?
  5. What should a technician do if the power state of a circuit is unclear?

Cleanup and Reset Instructions

  1. Leave all power sources disconnected.
  2. Return tools to the correct location.
  3. Return parts to the parts tray unless the instructor asks you to leave the station as-is for review.
  4. Remove only the staged hazards the instructor tells you to reset.
  5. Clear the bench so the next student can inspect it.
  6. Report any damaged or confusing props to the instructor.
