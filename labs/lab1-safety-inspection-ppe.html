Lab 1: Safety Inspection and PPE

Week 1 required lab. Practice safe work habits before building circuits.

Week 1Lesson 1Lesson 2Week 1 Quiz

Safety Warning

No energized work: This inspection lab uses disconnected training equipment only. Do not touch wall outlets, unknown power supplies, or energized equipment.

Purpose and Skills Practiced

  • Identify common workstation hazards.
  • Select appropriate PPE.
  • Document corrective actions.
  • Practice the technician habit of inspecting before working.

Tools and Parts

  • Safety glasses
  • Disconnected breadboard or training board
  • Jumper wires and sample components
  • Staged hazard cards or instructor-prepared workstation
  • Lab worksheet or notebook

Setup Diagram

Training bench inspection zones:
[Tools] [Parts tray] [Training board] [Power source removed] [Notebook]
Look for: clutter, exposed conductors, damaged leads, liquids, wrong PPE, loose wires.

Procedure

  1. Put on safety glasses before approaching the station.
  2. Confirm the training board is disconnected from all power.
  3. Inspect the bench for clutter, liquids, damaged wires, exposed conductors, and misplaced tools.
  4. Identify which PPE is required for the activity.
  5. Record each hazard and a safe corrective action.
  6. Ask the instructor to review your findings before resetting the station.

Troubleshooting Questions

  1. Why is a disconnected power source still part of the safety inspection?
  2. Which hazard would make you stop work immediately?
  3. What is one hazard that could damage components but not injure a person?

Reflection

What safety habit will you use before every electronics lab?

Cleanup and Reset

  1. Return tools to the correct location.
  2. Remove staged hazards only after instructor review.
  3. Reset components for the next group.
  4. Leave the station powered off.
